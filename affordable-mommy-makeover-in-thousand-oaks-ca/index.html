All cosmetic surgeons are not the same

In fact, the American Board of Plastic Surgery, and not the American Board of Cosmetic Surgery, is the board recognized by the American Board of Medical Specialists.

Surgeons board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery have at least 5-6 years of surgery training, and often 7 or 8 years or more. They have also undergone rigorous testing of their competence.

They are also unlikey to have hospital privileges in cosmetic surgery procedures, because hospitals are very careful to scrutinize the training and competence of their surgeons.

If you are seeking a surgeon with the most rigorous and extensive surgical training, for a plastic/cosmetic surgery procedure, you should seek a surgeon certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery.

Treatment for Post Operative Scars

Scar therapy post operatively is an interesting topic because surgeons have varying opinions, patients have their ideas and the scar cream industry would like you believe that their solution is the best.

Therefore, you will see and hear of many types of treatment. Let’s look at the science first. There is very little controlled, quality research to suggest what will work the best but there is evidence form the burn literature that helps answer some questions. In essence, the things that will help a scar look better faster are pressure, moisture and massage.

Thus anything like a silicone strip or sheet, silicone tape, regular tape, silicone cream that adds a film over the scar and even moisturizer will add moisture to your scars and help mature them faster. The more occlusive the product the better it will work.

You can also massage and that will help. Applying pressure is a little harder to do and not really necessary. I like the silicone creams or silicone tape as they are the easiest to deal with. You can start using any of these as soon as you are healed (the wounds are clean and dry, without scabs)
