When to Remove Stiches

Sutures should be removed at seven days to help reduce permanent lines, known as rail road tracks. Removal, ultimately depends on when the wound is ready, and it’s location. The longer you wait to remove the stitches, the harder they will be to get out, as the skin tends to grow over them

it is impossible to tell what effect delay on suture removal will have in your individual situation. There many different techniques of wound closure, and given intervalsof suture removal for each type of closure. I am sure that if your surgeon felt strongly, he/she would have told you so

Remove skin sutures within seven days after tummy tuck

Few of us use sutures that need to be removed, and fewer still a suture that can be seen on the surface of the skin. If you did have a ‘surface suture’ it is true that the sutures should be removed within seven days to avoid track marks on the skin

For any operation, it is desirable to remove the sutures as soon as initial wound healing has happened, so that the suture crossing the skin doesn’t create lines, and the actual suture holes don’t become epithelialized and leaver permanent spots
