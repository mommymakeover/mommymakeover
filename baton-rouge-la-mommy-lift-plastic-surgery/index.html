What is a Mommy Makeover?

The term Mommy Makeover in Baton Rouge includes combining cosmetic enhancement of the breasts and tummy in one operation.

The name Mommy Makeover comes from the common request for restoration (“makeover”) of the aesthetics of the breasts and abdomen after the changes to those body parts after pregnancy.

The breast procedures may entail any of the following individually or in combination:

– breast augmentation
– implants
– fat grafting
– breast lift
– breast reduction
– nipple-areola enhancement/reduction/repositioning
– release of retracted nipple

The tummy procedures may include:

– abdominoplasty – numerous techniques, from mini- to full- to extended
– C-section scar revision
– liposuction
– reshaping of the umbilicus (belly button)
– treatment of stretch marks

Tummy tuck + breast lift = mommy makeover

The combination of a tummy tuck and breast surgery (lift, augmentation, or both) is called a mommy makeover in Baton Rouge and doing them in a single stage is usually best.

This assumes that you are in good health, appropriate weight, and a nonsmoker.

When should I have my mommy makeover?

The timing of your mommy makeover in Baton Rouge depends on many separate factors. Six weeks is way too early. You need to get down to a stable weight close to your goal.

If you are breast feeding and plan on getting breast work, you need to wait until your breasts stop lactating. Most importantly this is the time to bond with your new baby. In reality, I would say six months to a year is more in line with the average.

Pregnancy and mommy makeover

If you are going to actively plan on getting pregnant at the same time you are planning your procedure, it would be more prudent to delay your procedure until after your pregnancy and when your body returns to the post-pregnancy baseline as your body will go through some additional changes with each pregnancy.

Baton Rouge Mommy Makeover before or after pregnancy

If you are planning on having another child in the next year, I recommend that you wait until after your final pregnancy to have a mommy makeover. By having a child after a mommy makeover, you will stretch your abdominal skin and fascia that were tightened during a tummy tuck.

This would likely require a “revision” to tighten your abdominal skin. In other words a second surgery will be an additional cost that you could otherwise avoid by waiting.

This is an easy decsion to make. I agree with the others in that you should finish having children before reshaping your body.
