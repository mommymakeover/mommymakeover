While I certainly understand the economic reasons for someone flying to the developing world for cosmetic surgery, it would be best for you to stay in that area for an extended period in the event that you have a problem.

Things come up. Sometimes they are small, sometimes more significant, but you want to be close to your doctor in those instances. He or she will want to make sure you do well.

Plastic surgery is a process and not an event, especially for a multi procedure surgery such as a Mommy Makeover in Bogota, Colombia. I understand the concept of “medical tourism”.

Sometimes, you’re choosing the best surgeon to do a particular procedure no matter what his or her location is. However, most of the time, medical tourism is a way of getting surgeries at a lower cost.

Unfortunately, if anything goes wrong and your surgeon is thousands of miles away from you, you may have a real problem. Realistically, Board Certified plastic surgeons in the New York City area may not want to take on responsibilities for surgeries that may not have gone well.
