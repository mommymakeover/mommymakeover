Mommy makeover procedures – cosmetic surgery

Most surgeons refer to a “mommy makeover” in Colombia as a series of cosmetic surgery procedures intended to restore a woman’s figure after pregnancy.

Procedures commonly performed during mommy makeovers include:
– Abominoplasty (tummy tuck)
– Breast Augmentation +/- Lift, or
– Breast Lift only without implants, liposuction

These procedures will not be covered by insurance

Medicaid coverage of abdominoplasty and breast augmentation

It is highly unlikely if Medicaid were to approve a tummy tuck. Several criteria including skin laxity, irritation (erythema intertrigo) skin breakdown, yeast infections, ulcers, ventral hernia, etc would need to be documented.

Even if this portion were approved, they would not pay for the rectus muscle repair which is completed in 80-90% of cases and is an essential component of aesthetic abdominoplasty

Forget about coverage for breast implants unless you have documented breast cancer or congenital deformity.

Understand that insurance companies love to deny anything even remotely cosmetic. They save money that way. It is doubtful you will get these operations covered under health insurance.

Insurance and mommy tuck

A tummy tuck and a breast augmentation are two operations that are elective cosmetic surgeries that as far as I know are not covered by insurance.

Medicaid was created on July 30, 1965, through Title XIX of the Social Security Act to provide BASIC care to needy (poor or disabled) American

While each states manages its own Medicaid program (giving rise to MediCal, MassHealth, TennCare etc), they are being monitored by the HUGE central agency , CMS, which manages Medicare – the health system for Americans over 65

Doctor Santiago Ruiz Mommy Plastic Surgery

Neither Medicare nor Medicaid allow or will pay for Cosmetic Surgery – operations done ONLY to improve appearance REGARDLESS of how much better it may make you feel. Surgeons who perform Cosmetic Surgery and bill these agencies are violating Federal laws and risking severe penalties

Medicaid / Medicare MAY pay for RECONSTRUCTIVE surgery, surgery which takes a damaged, deformed part of the body and restore it to normal. Making breasts larger (Breast Augmentation) and removing loose skin and flattening a poochy tummy (Tummy Tuck) are Cosmetic and will NOT be paid for by these agencies nor by all commercial insurers

Breast augmentation and abdominoplasty are cosmetic procedures

Insurance companies will often determine coverage based on whether or not a procedure is medically necessary. This is as opposed to cosmetic procedures, such as breast augmentation and abdominoplasty, which are not medically necessary and not covered
