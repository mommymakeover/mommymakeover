What is recovery like with a mommy makeover?

You have a very wrong and scary idea of Mommy Make Over procedures as well of the need for and the use of pain medication after surgery.

The words “comatose”, “(in)coherent” and “so much pain as to scare my children” should not be in your recovery vocabulary much less thinking.

Plastic surgeons use many ways to make these procedures less painful, numbing the muscle lining or using pain pumps.

Consider a pain pump

Having a mommy makeover like you are planning is a relatively common procedure these days. and combining these surgeries can lead to an operation that takes anywhere from 4 to 6 hours or so.

I would consider a couple of things – talk to your surgeon about possibly using a pain pump.

Recovery after mommy makeover week after

This is a catheter that is placed in the operating room which slowly drips in local anesthetic (like novocaine you get at the dentist) directly to the areas that will be most sore after the surgery. They have been shown in studies to decrease the need for pain medicine by mouth without making you drowsy.

That being said, with small children, i usually recommend that you get some help at home for a week or two after the surgery – friend or family who can come help you with the kids and make your life easier! (Eric Chang, MD, Baltimore Plastic Surgeon)

Great Pain Control Makes Recovery Much Easier

Recovery after mommy makeover in 2 days

The latest innovation in pain control, the pain pump has dramatically reduced the discomfort after tummy tuck and breast augmentation, the two procedures that are usually done in a Mommy Makeover.

The pain pump uses small catheters placed during the surgery to slowly drip local anesthetic into the surgery sites, providing local anesthetic (numbing medicine) directly where it hurts. The result is a dramatic reduction in pain, so much so that our patients take less than half the pain medicine and report much greater comfort. You will not need to be comatose! Even though we can minimize the post op pain, to recover properly, you must plan to have someone to care for you and your children for at least 1 week after surgery.

To recover properly, you should avoid lifting more than ~20 pounds for about 6 weeks post-op. If necessary, you may lift your children carefully. (Paul C. Zwiebel, MD, Denver Plastic Surgeon)
