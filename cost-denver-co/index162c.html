Mommy Makeover Cost Denver CO

A Mommy Makeover in Denver CO is a combination of procedures to improve your body after pregnancy and usually includes treatment of the breasts, tummy, and body.

The breasts can be reduced, lifted, or enlarged – sometimes a combination of procedures is necessary.

The tummy may be treated by a tummy tuck – sometimes a mini-tuck or just liposuction will do. And, liposuction of the remainder of the body.

Each individual has their own desires and their plastic surgeon can recommend what would be best. T

he time for surgery and recovery depends upon what actually is done, of course, but can run from 1-5 hours.

A Mommy Makeover can be done with twilight anesthesia and you can go home the same day! (Randy J. Buckspan, MD, Austin Plastic Surgeon)

Mommy Makeovers are not the same for every woman

By definition, a Denver CO Mommy Makeover is usually a combination of procedures done at the same time.

One of the more common combinations involves breast and tummy contouring together. Other women choose to have abdominal and thigh contouring, or breast and arm contouring.

Others choose breast or tummy and facial contouring. Just talk with your surgeon about your concerns, and she will help you put together a plan that’s safe for you. (Carmen Kavali, MD, Atlanta Plastic Surgeon)

I perform at least one “Mommy Makeover” almost everyday of my practice and I never get tired of seeing the incredible change that occurs with correction of so many of the effects of pregnancy. Breast surgery (augmentation or lift) and tummy tucks are the two most common procedures performed and are usually performed simultaneously. I do a procedure called an “extended abdominoplasty” that is exactly as you described…. extending the tummy tuck scar around the sides towards the back to remove either stretch marks , extra skin or both.

Dr. Frank E. Campanile, MD Mommy Tummy Tuck

If extended further towards the back , I refer to it as a “flankplasty” and if it goes all the way around the back I refer to it as a “belt Lipectomy” or a “body lift”. The limitation in removal of stretch marks along the sides is based mostly on how high they extend up the sides; complete removal may leave scars too high which may be aesthetically displeasing.

I do agree with you that if it is possible to remove stretch marks, the scar of the tummy tuck can be much better than the stretch marks left behind. Exactly what you will need can be determined at a consultation. (M. Bradley Calobrace, MD, FACS, Louisville Plastic Surgeon)
