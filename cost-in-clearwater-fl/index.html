Recovery from Mommy Makeover

Dr. Melinda Lirag Lacerna-Kimbrell Tummy Tuck Lipo And Breast Augmentation

As most people know, the Mommy Makeover includes simultaneous surgery on the breasts and abdominal area that have been affected by pregnancy. Breast surgery may include a lift, augmentation, combined augmentation/lift, or breast reduction.

Abdominal surgery may include abdominoplasty (tummy tuck), liposuction or a combination of tummy tuck and liposuction.

Factors affecting recovery include the number of procedures, aggressiveness of procedures, such as extensive liposuctioning or tightening of the waistline, baseline pain tolerance, and level of activity.

One of the nicest things your husband can do for you is book a few days at a local hotel for you and a friend/caretaker, while he watches your children.

While walking early and often after this surgery is essential to prevent blood clots, lifting your 2 year-old is no fun, and may cause problems such as pain, wound separation, bleeding and swelling.

In addition to taking it easy, good pain medication, use of Valium as a muscle relaxer, local muscles blocks, pain pumps, and support garments can make your experience much easier. The majority of patients are back on their feet in 2 weeks, although it generally takes 4-6 weeks to be back to normal activity (including lifting those little ones). (Karen Vaniver, MD, Kennewick Plastic Surgeon)

I keep my patients overnight for a number of reasons, but it is not altogether necessary. The risk for significant infection, blood transfusion, or major complication is very small.

With an abdominoplasty, smaller problems are more common. Seromas, or collections of fluid under the skin are fairly common. They are tapped off in the doctor’s office.
