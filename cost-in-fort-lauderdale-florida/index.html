Mommy Body Makeover In Fort Lauderdale

Mommy makeover in Fort Lauderdale is a term given to any group of operations that are intended to correct some of the unwanted body changes occurring after pregnancy and breast feeding. Usually, some type of abdominal recontouring is included.

A full abdominoplasty is the most extensive procedure performed in the usual combination of procedures described as a mommy makeover. A full abdominoplasty starts with an incision that goes from one hip to the other.

This is normally done low enough to be concealed with a two piece bathing suit. The incision goes through the skin and fat layer down to the muscle layer. At this level, the skin and fat layer are lifted off of the muscle layer up to the belly button.

At this point, the belly button is released from the skin so that the skin can continue to be elevated up to the rib cage. This leaves the belly button still sitting on top of the muscle layer.

After tightening up the muscle layer with stitches, the skin and fat are pulled tight down over the lower incision and the excess skin and fat are removed. If a full abdominoplasty is performed normally, then heavy housekeeping work such as mopping, vacuuming and scrubbing should be avoided for the first month.

If a lesser procedure such as liposuction only is performed on the abdomen, then return to these activities could occur within seven to ten days. Normally, a mommy makeover includes some type of breast surgery. This type of surgery could either be breast enlargement, a breast lift, or a combination of both.

Occasionally, a breast reduction is performed. When this type of surgery is performed by itself, the patients are advised to restrict their upper body activity for three to four weeks.

Surgical Drains After Tummy Tuck and Mommy Makeover

Dr. Robert E. Rothfield Recovery From Mommy Makeover

Drains are left in place after full tummy tuck in order to remove any buildup of swelling fluid under the skin. In general, the drains can be removed at 5 to 7 days. The concept of having a drain sounds strange to patients at first, but as you will see after your surgery, the drains really are not a big deal and too much concern is placed on the concept of the drains prior to surgery. Assuming you are seeing a board certified plastic surgeon, my best advice is to trust your surgeon and not worry.

The drains would normally be used for the tummy tuck portion of the procedure. The length of time they need to remain in place varies. Removal usually is determined by the amount and color of the drainage. The color of the drainage should be a clear yellow or light pink. The amount of drainage should be under a certain number of cc’s. I usually wait until there is less than 30cc’s in each drain for a 24-hour period. (This amount may be different for different physicians.) Generally the amount of time, if everything goes smoothly, is less than 1 week.

Improving scars after mommy makeover

I treat postoperative incisions with paper tape or steri-strips for 3-4 weeks. Silicone sheeting or a product such as scar guard is used following removal of the tape. Scar massage also helps.

Recovery after a mommy make over

You should plan to decrease your activity level for at least the first 2 weeks. From the third to the fourth week you should be building up to more normal activities.

Timing Mommy Makeover after Nursing

I recommend my patients to consider breast surgery (augmentation, or lift) 6 months after the last breast feeding; same is true for a tummy tuck. The reason to wait is to perform surgery after the effects of hormones of pregnancy have subsided and the tissues have returned to their normal status. Early surgery on breasts may result in cysts in the milk ducts which might still have some residual milk. It has been shown that it takes tissues and milk ducts up to 6 months to return to their normal state.
