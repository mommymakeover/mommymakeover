Mommy Plastic Surgery in Grand Rapids, MI

Every mom is different, every body is different.

A mommy makeover in Grand Rapids is a combination procedure aimed at correcting many of the changes that occur in a woman’s body from the stresses of pregnancy.

The exact procedures can be different from one mom to another! From the nature of what happens with pregnancy it almost always includes some form of abdominal re-contouring to counteract the stretched skin and separated muscles.

A tummy tuck is usually the right answer to address those problems.Many people also need breast enhancement with an implant and or lifting, while others have the opposite problem of too much breast tissue after kids, and then a breast reduction can help.

Finally most moms need at least a little liposuction (of hips, thighs, possibly back or arms) to fine tune their contour… even someone at a good weight for their height can have pockets of fat in undesirable places!Liposuction helps shape those areas and bring everything into proportion.

Chances are that if you are already considering a tummy tuck after your kids, you would also benefit from these other areas at the same time.

The really great part about a mommy makeover is that it gets all done in one surgery, one anesthetic, and one downtime… and it is less expensive than doing each component separately!

That’s a win-win situation! If you are interested find a board certified plastic surgeon in your area to help you choose the approach that is best for you. (Marie E. Montag, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

Recovery from a Mommy Makeover

Mommy makeover’s are uncomfortable because of the abdominoplasty. It is the tightening of the fascia (that encases your muscles) that causes the most discomfort.

Most patients are feeling much better after 2 weeks.

In our practice we use what is called a pain pump to help decrease the initial pain.

This assists the pain medication for the first 48-72 hours. Everyone handles pain differently however.

Some patients require significantly more pain medicine than others but you should not ever be incoherent!

Everyone does recover eventually and most are back to all normal activities in 6 weeks. (Ellen A. Janetzke, MD, Birmingham Plastic Surgeon)

While every patient is different in their healing capabilities and pain tolerance, in general, after a “mommy makeover” you can expect to need oral pain medications for 5-8 days.

I find that most patients who have had a C-setion in the past, consider a tummy tuck to be comparable in pain/recovery.

That being said, you will not be able to do housework, heavy lifting, pick up your children for at least 2 weeks or so. But you will not be, nor should be, lying in bed all day.

After the first 2-3 days you should be able to move around better, shower, dress yourself, etc. (Niki Christopoulos, MD, FACS, Chicago Plastic Surgeon)
