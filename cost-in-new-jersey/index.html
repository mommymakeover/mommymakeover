Mommy Makeover Surgery – Different types

“Mommy Makeover in New Jersey” typically refers to combined breast enhancement and abdominal contour improvement.

Surgical time varies depending on the required surgery and the surgeon’s speed.

Some examples are:

– Breast Augmentation and Tummy Tuck – 3-4 hours
– Breast Lift and Tummy Tuck – 4-5 hours
– Breast Reduction and Tummy Tuck – 5-6 hours

I do not like performing mommy lift that is expected to last more than 6 hours due to the increased risks associated with longer operative times.

For some procedural combinations, it is smart to stage the operations. In some cases, predicted long operative times might be the reason to stage the operations.

In other cases, anatomic constraints and blood supply issues may make it smart to stage operations (such as breast lift and breast augmentation surgery.)

The discomfort of a mommy makeover is mostly related to the abdominal component.

Breast surgery does hurt, but it normally does not add that much compared to the discomfort of a tummy tuck.

This makes combining the procedures attractive, as you seem to get “something for nothing” (in terms of discomfort.)

Combining procedures does result in a notable cost savings. You do not have to pay for the redundant start-up costs for anesthesia or the operating room, and the fact that there is only one recovery period is also beneficial.

The time off work for yourself or caregivers should be considered when making surgical plans. Of course the most important issue with any surgery is safety, so no matter how attractive it sounds to combine operations, if it is not safe for some reason – don’t do it.

It is a very normal occurrence to have early weight gain after major surgery. The body goes into a “post trauma” mode and retains fluids for a period of time following plastic surgery after baby.

This length of time varies from individual to individual.
