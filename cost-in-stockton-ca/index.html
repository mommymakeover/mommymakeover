Recovering from a Mommy Makeover

A “Mommy Makeover” is a combination of procedures designed to restore your body to the condition it was in prior to the birth of your children. There are many different varieties of mommy makeovers, so be sure to clarify with your surgeon the procedures you are having.

The most common procedures are a combination of a breast surgery, such as an augmentation or breast lift, or both, and a body contouring procedure, such as an abdominoplasty (tummy tuck). The breasts and the belly the most affected areas after childbirth and breastfeeding. With the combination of procedures, the longest part of the recovery is from the tummy tuck. I ask my patients to avoid strenuous activity for 2-3 weeks and no heavy lifting for 6 weeks.

In terms of pain, the use of pain pumps (which I use in all of my tummy tuck patients) has significantly cut down the amount of pain medications needed. After 1 week, you should no longer need narcotic pain medication (the medications most likely to cause you feelings of sedation. The first week of recovery will be the most challenging, but certainly not unbearable. If you are only having liposuction, recovery will be shorter. Recovery from a breast augmentation or lift is only 2 weeks, and back to full activity after 3 weeks. You will love getting your body back in shape! (Scott J. Engel, MD, FACS, Sarasota Plastic Surgeon)

Recovery from a Mommy Makeover

Combining plastic surgical procedures is a very common practice. In particular, the “mommy makeover” procedure consists of a tummy tuck and some sort of breast procedure. You have written that your are having an augmentation mastopexy with your tummy tuck.

Between the two, the tummy tuck has a longer recovery period. With a tummy tuck, the majority of the discomfort comes from two parts of the procedure – muscle tightening and large amounts of liposuction.

Typically, patients have a “tight” feeling from the closure of the abdominal skin which gradually subsides over a few weeks.The post-surgical pain lasts anywhere from 3 days to two weeks but improves significantly in the first week. For the breast procedure, the postoperative discomfort is very well tolerated with most patients on over-the-counter pain medications by the third or fourth day.

As far as full activity and heavy lifting (ie your kids), I advise people to wait 3 to 4 weeks if they’ve had muscle tightening, 2 weeks if no muscle tightening. (David Bogue, MD, Boca Raton Plastic Surgeon)
