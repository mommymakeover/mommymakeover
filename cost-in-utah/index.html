Plastic Surgery after Childbirth: Mommy Makeover in Utah

The term “mommy makeover” generallly refers to several procedures which target changes to your body after pregnency and childbirth.

These procedures usually include a tummy tuck, breast lift or augmentation, and liposuction.

With preganancy the abdominal muscles stretch out to accomodate the enlarging uterus. The mucles charges are termed rectus diastasis.

The skin around the belly button (umbilicus) and in the lower abdomen also stretches which can result in permanent stretch marks. A tummy tuck incision is designed to be very low on the abdomen so that it could be covered in most bathing suits.

Skin from the belly button to the just above the pubic area is removed. The abdominal muscles are also repaired to restore the waistline and narrow the waist. With pregnancy, women will store fat in their hips, legs, and love handle area (flanks).

This fat is best treated with liposuction where the excess is removed to restore the prepregnancy shape in these problem areas.

Pregnancy and breast feeding also enlarge the breasts and stretch out the skin over the breasts.

After breasts feeding the breasts will become smaller (involute) but the skin will not shrink. This cases the breasts to look droopy or deflated. In most cases, the breasts can be enhanced with an implant which restores the lost volume and gives a small lifting effect.

A Tummy Tuck With Breast Enhancement Surgery Recovery

In more severe, cases a breast lift is necessary to restore the natural tear drop shape of the breast.

The lift can be done alone or with an implant. These procedures can safely be done at the same time in most cases.

Housekeeping Work After a Mommy Makeover Surgery

Your concern as to when you can get back to work following your Mommy Makeover procedure is a common one and unfortunately is highly variable and depends largely on what type of work a patient does.

Most patients are advised to take off roughly 7-10 days from work, but for patients with more strenuous or physical jobs (including housekeeping) this time off requirement is often increased (even doubled).  I would recommend discussing how much time off from work is recommending considering your line of work with your operating surgeon.
