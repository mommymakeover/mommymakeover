In general, Mommy make over in Des Moines is done when a combination of tummytuck, breast augmentation, liposuction and breast lift are done at the same time.

Most patients after pregnancy will have excess skin of the breast and some sort of breast lift is needed.

Having said that, there are many patients that can have breast augmentation alone with excellent result. Consider having a personalized consultation with a board certified plastic surgeon to review all avaiable options to create your desired outcome.

The basic elements of Des Moines mommy makeover are a breast procedure tailored to your specific needs, an abdominoplasty and some additional liposuction, if needed.

You can choose a part of this if you wish. The end result, however, will be less than optimal without the entire procedures being performed.

Mommy make-over but no breast lift

You can definitely not have a lift if you don’t need or want it!A mommy make-over is a tummy tuck with breast implants, OR Breast lift Or breast reduction OR breast lift + implants – depending on what is needed.

A tummy tuck with breast augmentation will take about 3 – 5 hours, depending on what needs to be done. It’s a great operation – most women are very pleased with their results!

Mommy makeover and children

If you are contemplating having children then I would highly recommend delaying your abdominoplasty. Pregnancy will have the biggest impact on your abdominal contour. It is reasonable to have your breast augmentation and fat transfer to your buttocks next summer and plan on delaying your tummy tuck until you are done having children.

How soon after mommy lift can I try for a baby?

Pregnancy following tummy tuck surgery is possible and is not dangerous, but if you plan on future pregnancies, you should wait to have mommy makeover in Des Moines until you are done having children, since pregnancy will stretch out the skin and abdominal muscles again, usually undoing the repairs that were achieved with the abdominoplasty.

In that case, a secondary tummy tuck to repair recurrent muscle laxity or loose skin may be beneficial. Most plastic surgeons suggest that you finish having children before undergoing an abdominoplasty with repair of the stretched out abdominal muscles

I would suggest that you discuss this and any concernsthat you have with your plastic surgeon who should be certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery and ideally a member of the American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery (ASAPS).

Down time after Mommy make over

The most important thing when considering these type of surgeries is your safety. It is very unrealistic that you will be able to go back to work after 5 days and if you did you would probably be compromising your safety. I strongly recommend that you organize yourself to allocate enough time for a proper recuperation from your mommy makeover.
