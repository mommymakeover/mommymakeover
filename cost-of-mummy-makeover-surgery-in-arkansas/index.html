If you do decide to do just one, my advice is to do that which bothers you more first, because you will feel better about yourself, and won’t regret not doing the other procedure as much. The majority of my patients tend to do both at the same time. Generally this also saves some money.

Breast Augmentation and Tummy Tuck (together) recovery is significantly less than each done separately (and less costly, too)!

These two operations are commonly performed together, not just to save money, time off work or parenting duties, and number of doctor visits for recheck, but also to reduce risks.

Neither mommy lift has significant risks, but however minimal they are, two operations doubles these risks!If you were talking about a 5-7 hour body lift plus a 3 hour breast lift, for example, then staging these into two procedures is reasonable, appropriate, and safer. but a 2-3 hour (at most) tummy tuck plus a 1 hour breast augmentation is not “pushing the envelope” and is quite easily recovered from as a combined operation.

In fact, the restrictions and return to activity and exercise guidelines I recommend for tummy tuck (alone) are EXACTLY THE SAME as tummy tuck plus breast augmentation recommendations. IMHO, you are doing neither yourself nor your children any favor by having two operations, two recoveries, and two sets of restrictions.
