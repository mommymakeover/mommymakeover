What is a Mommy Makeover in Melbourne?

This popular term refers to women who are having cosmetic surgery to regain their pre-pregnancy body. It can be a variety of usually 2 to 3 surgeries combined.

Mainly it is a full tummy tuck, some type of breast surgery such as; breast augmentation, breast lift (or both) and sometimes it could be breast reduction surgery

In addition, there is usually some liposuction done. However, it is an individualized procedure depneding on what the woman needs.

Will doing a breast lift at the same time as a tummy tuck affect the quality and attention to detail of each?

In general, combining a tummy tuck and breast lift should not affect the quality of the result of either procedure. It is quite common to combine these procedures and should not cause your surgeon to rush through the surgery

When combining cosmetic procedures, it has been shown that surgeries that take longer than 6 hours to perform can result in somewhat higher complication rates. But, in most cases, these two procedures can be performed within this time restraint

Check with your surgeon for their recommendations in your case.

Smoking cessation

In our practice we advise our patients to cease smoking as soon as possible, but a minimum of 2 weeks prior to surgery, as smoking increases all risks associated with surgery to include but not be limited to increased risk of bleeding, bruising, delayed wound healing, infection, capsular contracture, and the potential need for additional surgery

We also advise our patients to avoid smoking or exposure to secondhand smoke for a minimum of 4 weeks after surgery.

First, it is ideal to wait until you’re done having children to have a tummy tuck. However, you are young, and you indicate that it may be 5-6 years before you have another child

You CAN have a baby after having a tummy tuck–you just “undo” some of the results (but not all of them). So the first question you need to answer for yourself is, “What kind of tummy do I want to live with until I decide if I’m having more children?” If the answer is, “a smaller version of what I have now, with some loose skin and all my stretch marks still being present”, then liposuction is your better option

There is less recovery from liposuction (any type of liposuction, not just laser-assisted) and less cost. And you haven’t burned any bridges–you can always have a tummy tuck later, if you want it. But, if the answer is “as flat and tight as possible”, then you need a tummy tuck

But which kind of tummy tuck? The second question to ask yourself is, “what bothers me about my tummy? Is it stretch marks, loose skin, the crinkling around the belly button, the lower abdominal bulge, extra fat?” If your answer includes anything related to stretch marks, loose skin, or the skin around your belly button, then you need a FULL tummy tuck

Doctor Eddie Cheng Mother Makeover

If your answer is only the bulge in the lower abdomen (meaning you’re perfectly happy with the skin on your upper abdomen and around your belly button), then you might consider a MINI-tummy tuck

I will caution you that very few women are actually good candidates for a mini-tummy tuck. And a mini-tummy tuck does not mean a mini-scar. The scar still needs to be as long as it needs to be to have a smooth result

Your best option is all about what YOU want to achieve.
