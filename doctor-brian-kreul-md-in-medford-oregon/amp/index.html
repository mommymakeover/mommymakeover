If I want to get Liposuction on my stomach, arms and thighs, will I have sagging skin?
If I want to get Liposuction on my stomach, arms and thighs, will I have sagging skin?
Sep 4, 2014
Liposuction works best to reduce fat in areas where there is mild excess. In younger people with good skin elasticity, the skin usually retracts. If the skin quality is poor, the skin may not contract and you can be left with sagging skin in the area. Also, the larger the volume of liposuction, the less likely the skin is to contract. Compression garments should be worn over the liposuction areas for at least 6 weeks after the procedure to help the skin contract.

When Does Swelling After Breast Augmentation Begin to Subside?
When Does Swelling After Breast Augmentation Begin to Subside?
Sep 4, 2014
Swelling after breast augmentation persists for 4-6 weeks at the least. Some patients may have swelling, firmness, and high implants for several months. There are many factors that may affect the length of time your swelling persists. Submuscular implants tend to stay higher, rounder, firmer, and more swollen for a longer period of time. Usually, you will have fairly good idea of your final bra size around 6-8 weeks after surgery.
Tummy Tuck Scars
I imagine that you can have tummy tuck scars hidden below your underpants but is this true? can i wear a bikini 1 year after tum
Sep 4, 2014
You definitely should have your scar below your bikini line after a tummy tuck. In most cases, it is possible to place the scar low at the pubis and hips so that it is concealed by any low cut garment you might wear. You will have a scar at the umbilicus, but typically that scar has faded to a thin white line at a year.

Should I wait until I lose more weight?
Sep 4, 2014
I think it is always safest to lose as much weight as possible before doing any surgical procedure. Certainly, if you plan to have a breast lift, you should lose all the weight you desire before surgery. Losing weight after a breast lift could result in loss of breast volume and sagging of the tissue. Obviously, this could adversely affect your result. If your plan is to just remove your implants, I think you could proceed with surgery at any time.
