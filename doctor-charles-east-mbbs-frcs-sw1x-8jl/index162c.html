not a polybeak

Hi,I believe your question relates to the shape of your tip?You have a low curved bridge and a round shaped projected tip. Polybeak (parrots beak) is where the tip settles back, and the tissues aboue the tip are too high showing the characteristic form.Hope this helps to explain.
Bulbous tip – why wouldn’t a revision surgery help?
secondary tip surgery
Jan 8, 2023
Hello,A drop of the nasal tip after surgery usually means a weakened support and is a common reason for revision surgery, Your images appear to show relatively normal skin so repositioning/ restructuing for a better shape would need a consultaion with a specialist rhinoplasty surgeon.Revisions are usually considered around 12 months after the last operation.
Can the depressor septi muscle be cut without having a full rhinoplasty? Concerned about droop
droopy tip
Jan 8, 2023
Hi, the smiling dynamic change causing a ‘droopy tip’ is often a combination of muscle factors- the depressor is rarely the cause so you need to consult a specialist rhinoplasty surgeon for proper assesment, evaluation and advice about your needs.
Can rhinoplasty cause a lower smile line/change in lip position?
lip position after rhinoplasty
Jan 8, 2023
Hello, this is a good question.The circular muscle of the upper lip is suspended from the base of the nose by vertival muscles and ligaments. The lip position can be lifted or lowered (gummy smile) in rhinoplasty thereby altering teeth visibility, so its important to discuss this issue with your surgeon when planning an operation.
