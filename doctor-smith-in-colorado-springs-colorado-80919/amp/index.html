Non-Board Certified Plastic Surgeon

Thank you for your very important question. If everything goes correctly and there are no unforeseen problems, a non-board certified plastic surgeon may be fine. Unfortunately, you never know what problems may be encountered during surgery. Board certified plastic surgeons have years of training and are held to a much higher standard.

Botox During Pregnancy

When is the right time to remove implants after surgery?
Breast Implant Removal
Jun 15, 2016
Thank you for your question.  It is normal for women to have pain for the first several weeks after surgery.  I typically recommend  patients to leave their implants in place for at least 3 months after surgery before having them removed.  I would recommend having a discussion with your plastic surgeon and make sure that you have given yourself time to heal before proceeding with implant removal.  Best of Luck!
Will my puckered scars flatten???
Breast Augmentation Scars
Jun 15, 2016
Thank you for your questions.  Your “puckering”  is most likely from the sutures below the incisions and will continue to improve.  They should flatten with time.  I don’t want to tell you that you will not require a scar revision, but it is unlikely and they should continue to improve.  Silicone gel sheets and scar massage will help your incisions to heal as best as they can.  Best of Luck!
Are my breast had drop yet?
Breast Implant Position
Jun 15, 2016
Thank you for your questions.  Being only 7 days out from surgery, your breasts have not yet settled into their final position.  I tell my patients that your breasts will cont. to settle for the first 3 months after surgery.  It is also difficult to determine your final size until your implants full settle.  Talk to your plastic surgeon about implant massage and expectations for settling.  Best of Luck.
How to get operative records?
Breast Implant Records
Jun 15, 2016
Each state has different requirements for how long a physician is required to keep their medical records.  If you are considering having your implants removed, I do not feel medical records would be required.  If you still would like to find out your implant information you may need to speak with the surgery center or even the implant company.  Best of Luck.
Any suggestions for tuberous breast?
Tuberous Breasts
Jun 15, 2016
Thank you for your questions.  There are options available to you but it all depends on your current breasts size and where you would like to go.  Tuberous breasts can be difficult and can often require multiple surgeries.  In my experience, implants offer the best results.  It is important to be seen by an experienced plastic surgeon.  Best of Luck.
Alloderm for nasolabial fold?
Nasolabial Fold
Jun 15, 2016
Thank you for your question.  Alloderm wouldn’t be my first choice to correct a nasolabial fold defect but that doesn’t mean that it can’t work.  I would recommend talking with your plastic surgeon and make sure you see their past results.  If you are happy with these results and feel comfortable, I feel it would be reasonable to proceed.  Just make sure you know all of your options (e.g. fat grafting, filler).  Best of Luck!
Brachioplasty – staples or sutures?
Staples with Arm Lift
Jun 15, 2016
Thank you for your question.  I do not use staples because I do not like the appearance of the scars.  Arm incisions do have a difficult time healing and often want to widen.  Your plastic surgeon may find that the final results are better with staples.  I would recommend having a discussion them him/her about why they choose the technique they did.  Best of luck!
Scar revision/fixing to chest wall, or laser to scars? 5 years post explantation
Scarring after
Apr 19, 2016
Thank you for your questions.  Based on your photos I feel a scar revision would give you the best result.  Laser therapy may help with the outward appearance of the scar but will not help improve the “tucked”  appearance of the scar.  Best of Luck.
Is there any way to lift breasts after a previous augmentation to a bigger size without a “lollipop”
Breast Lift after a Breast Augmentation
Apr 19, 2016
Thank you for your questions.  Yes, a breast lift can be performed after a breast augmentation has been performed.  The type of lift that is required depends on how far your nipples and areola need to be moved.  In some instances, a small lift can be ached with minimal scarring above or completely around the areola.  Best of Luck.
