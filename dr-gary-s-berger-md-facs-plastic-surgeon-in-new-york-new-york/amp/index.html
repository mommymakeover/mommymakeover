What procedures are best for me to avoid messing up my breast tattoos?
Go for it!
May 24, 2020
You can have a lift and/or reduction and not really change the tattoos that much, but the nipple on the left may be next to or in the middle of that one. A plastic surgeon can draw it on you with a marker so you can see what would change.
What is the best procedure for me? I want a tummy tuck and breast lift
Mommy Makeover
May 24, 2020
You would have better long term results and safer surgery if you lowered your BMI. If you can’t really do that, find a board certified plastic surgeon who is experienced with higher BMI patients. A full tummy tuck may not really make much difference. Doing an extended panniculectomy (removing the hanging part) with a lot of lipo might be a faster recovery and a safer surgery for you. Only see a board certified plastic surgeon. Good luck!
Will it make a lot of difference if I lose 25 pounds before the procedure?
Goal weight
May 24, 2020
If you said that you were never going to get down to your goal weight, I would say go for it. But if you have it in you to take off the weight, then being at your goal weight is a better choice. (Price isn’t really affected by 25lbs in most offices.)
Am I a candidate for a mommy makeover? 219 lb, 5’3″, c-sections.
Better to lose weight but you have options
May 24, 2020
Ideally, you would lose weight first. But if that’s really not happening, a board certified plastci surgeon can help you evaluate your options including liposuction and tummy tuck. A mommy makeover usually also includes breast surgery, but if you don’t want that, so be it. It is hard to be more specific without more revealing pictures, to be honest
Good luck
I am obese and want to know if fat transfer is an option for me?
You do have options
May 24, 2020
In a perfect world, you would reach your goal weight before surgery. However, there are people who can’t get the needle to budge. You can still have liposuction to improve your appearance in clothing. Doing it at a higher weight puts you at a higher risk, as does fat transfer. But it is definitely doable! See only a Board certified experienced plastic surgeon!
Good luck!
Are this breasts tubular and can I solve them without implants?
Not tuebrous
May 24, 2020
In answer to your question, you do not have tuberous breasts. Unfortunately, they are different in shape and appearance. This can be made better with iimplants. An inverted nipple can also be improved. See only a board-certified plastic surgeon to evaluate your options!
Do I have tuberous breasts? Would surgery be able to correct this?
Small not tuberous
May 24, 2020
You don’t have tuberous breasts. But what likely bothers you can only be corrected with implants. There are smaller ones. A board certified plastic surgeon can help you figure it out. Good luck!
I’m 56 African American, 122 lbs, 5’6 ready for breast implants, am I too old?

Can I benefit from a BA without a lift?
Lift would help
May 19, 2020
You are a great candidate to put the implant in and do a lift. But if that isn’t what you want, review photos with a surgeon who can show you realistically what you would look like without the lift. You may be thrilled in a bathing suit, but less so when you are undressed.
Thin patient wanting a BBL. Advice?
Minimal change
May 19, 2020
Make sure to see a board certified plastic surgeon in person, who can examine the small amount of fat that you have to see whether it will really make a difference. You can’t put more in than we get out!
Good luck!
