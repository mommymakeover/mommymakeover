Breast Implants are 34 yrs old, Should they be replaced?
Breast Implants are 34 yrs old, Should they be replaced?
Feb 25, 2020
Double lumen implants have an outer layer of saline that surrounds the inner implant of silicone.  It is highly likely in 34 year old implants that the outer shell that contains the saline has deflated.  The inner shell containing the silicone also has a high likelihood of leaking.  I would recommend you have the implants and the capsules removed.  If you wish, you could leave the implants out or replace the implants with either saline or silicone implants. Best wishes.

What are my options and possible results regarding removal of my ruptured implants?
What are my options and possible results regarding removal of my ruptured implants?
Feb 21, 2020
With ruptured implants, it is best to have the implants removed along with the surrounding capsule.  You may wish to leave out the implants or you could replace them.  The implant manufacturer will provide implants, for ruptured implants, should you wish to replace them.  Your warranty for additional monetary assistance from the manufacturer ends at 10 years.  Consult with plastic surgeons who can give you specific recommendations for surgery for you.  Best wishes.

When getting breast implants removed will there just be an empty pocket? Is there concern for an air embolism?
Feb 21, 2020
Once the implants (and part or all of the capsules)  are removed, the breast tissue settles and adheres or grows back together.  The air that was in the pocket is removed by surgical drains, and not absorbed into the vascular system (an embolism).

Should I go with en bloc for my implant removal? I have severe CC on the left and want a lift.
Feb 19, 2020
You may want removal of your implants and capsule in one piece, en bloc, especially if your implant is leaking or ruptured.  Some patients wish to have en bloc removal due to  medical conditions arising from their implants.  Other patients want a complete capsulectomy to remove all of the thickened capsule. Why must a thickened capsule be removed for explant?
Why must a thickened capsule be removed for explant?
Feb 19, 2020
A thickened capsule from a grade III encapsulation is best removed.  If the thickened capsule is left after implant removal, the capsule left behind may leave a visual deformity or palpable mass.  The retained capsule may increase the likelihood of fluid buildup-seroma.  Very little breast tissue if any will be removed with removal of the capsule.  Hope this helps. Best wishes.
