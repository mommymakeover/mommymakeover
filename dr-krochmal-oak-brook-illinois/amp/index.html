Statement: Dr. Daniel Krochmal is a double board-certified plastic surgeon and the founder of The Krochmal Center for Plastic Surgery, serving patients in the Greater Chicago Area. Known for his attention to detail, concierge-level service, and commitment to patient safety, Dr. Krochmal specializes in breast augmentation, tummy tucks, liposuction, and complete mommy makeovers.

He also has expertise in both surgical and nonsurgical facial rejuvenation procedures, from facelifts, rhinoplasty, and eyelid surgery (blepharoplasty) to Silhouette InstaLift thread lifts, dermal fillers, and Botox. He also offers popular nonsurgical fat reduction procedures such as CoolSculpting and Vanquish.

In addition to benefiting from Dr. Krochmal’s experience and skill, patients love Dr. Krochmal’s quirky sense of humor and commitment to patients’ overall care. “I prioritize offering customized, caring, concierge-level service to all of my patients,” says Dr. Krochmal. “I’m also intensely active in patient safety conversations and committees. Their well being through this process is my top priority.”

A Michigan native, Dr. Krochmal received his medical degree from the University of Michigan Medical School in Ann Arbor. He went on to complete a General Surgery residency at the Mayo Clinic in Arizona and a Plastic Surgery residency at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, before training at the prestigious Hunstad Kortesis Center in Charlotte, where he learned advanced body contouring, breast, and facial aesthetic procedures.

Today, Dr. Krochmal continues to be an active researcher in the field of plastic surgery, and is engaged in projects involving DVT (deep vein thrombosis) blood clot reduction following abdominoplasty, safety in buttocks fat grafting procedures, and outcomes for large volume liposuction for lipedema.

A passionate advocate for patient safety and healthcare access, he sits on the Legislative Advocacy Committee for the American Society of Plastic Surgeons (ASPS) as well as the Patient Safety Committees for ASPS and the American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery (ASAPS). Dr. Krochmal is also an active Fellow of the American College of Surgeons (FACS).

How to Convince Insurance Company to Cover Tummy Tuck?
Insurance
Feb 26, 2019
Thank you for your question.  An insurance company may cover the removal of excess skin below your belly button (called a “panniculectomy”) if you have documented chronic infections, or a history of massive weight loss if that’s part of your benefts.  They typically don’t cover a full “abdominoplasty” (repairing the muscles with repositioning the belly button).  I would contact your insurance company to discuss your benefits.Best,Dr. Dan KrochmalMAE Plastic SurgeryNorthbrook, IL
