Statement: Dr. Nikesh K.Patel is a leading, board-certified plastic surgeon and the founder of New Reflections Plastic Surgery in Freehold, New Jersey. An expert in procedures such as labiaplasty, tummy tuck, breast augmentation, and liposuction, he brings world-class knowledge and skill to his practice.

“I believe all procedures, whether simple or complex, require finesse and attention to detail,” says Dr. Patel. “This approach can lead to a refined result that looks natural and highlights your own beauty and personality.”

Dr. Patel received his bachelor’s degrees in Biomedical Materials Science at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. While in college, he participated in several research projects as a National Science Foundation/Engineering Research Center Research Fellow at Duke University in Durham, North Carolina. Dr. Patel later graduated from Wake Forest School of Medicine in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, where he performed research in the cardiac surgery and plastic surgery divisions.

He went on to complete years of rigorous training, including residencies in general surgery at Robert Wood Johnson Medical School – Rutgers in New Brunswick, New Jersey and plastic surgery at the Albany Medical Center in New York. During this period, Dr. Patel has also given back by joining medical mission trips to the Dominican Republic, providing surgical care for a variety of concerns including keloids, burn reconstruction, and cleft lip and palate deformities.

Dr. Patel has also completed a prestigious 12-month fellowship in advanced cosmetic surgery at the Cleveland Clinic in Ohio, to complete his training and education and hone his expertise.

A RealSelf Top Doctor, Dr. Patel has authored articles on the latest innovations of plastic surgery and presented his research at the European Society of Plastic Surgery meeting in Ghent, Belgium. He is an active member of the American Society of Plastic Surgeons, American Society of Aesthetic Plastic Surgeons and New Jersey Society of Plastic Surgeons. He is board certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery.

I have a bladder prolapse, how can a tummy tuck help this? Beneficial in any way?
I have a bladder prolapse, how can a tummy tuck help this? Beneficial in any way?
Apr 22, 2020
A medical correction of bladder prolapse (cystocele) should be addressed prior to having  an elective abdominoplasty procedure.  An abdominoplasty will not resolve this issue.   I would recommend you see your OB/GYN first and after the cystocele is repaired.  Once you are cleared by your OB/GYN, schedule a consultation with a board certified plastic surgeon to discuss your options for an abdominoplasty.  Best of luck!

Would I be able to get a tummy tuck even with my previous c-sections?
Apr 22, 2020
It is very common to have a tummy tuck after multiple c-sections.   During the tummy tuck procedure the prior c-section scars are removed as well as excess skin and fat.  You should make sure you are done having kids before pursing this procedure.  An in person consultation is always best to evaluate your options.  I would suggest to schedule a consultation with a board certified plastic surgeon with experience in this procedure to evaluate your options.

Would a breast lift be necessary or can fat transfer give more volume? (No implants)
Would a breast lift be necessary or can fat transfer give more volume? (No implants) (photo)
Mar 16, 2020
Based on your pictures it appears that you would most benefit from a breast lift and not just fat transfer alone.   Adding more volume to the breast without a lift will only increase the sag due to the extra weight.   A lift will allow for resizing of your nipple and areola, elevate your breast tissue, and improve shape.  Seek a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon and schedule a consultation to discuss your options.
How long is the usual time to take off work after a mommy makeover?
How long is the usual time to take off work after a mommy makeover?
Mar 11, 2020
The typical "mommy makeover" procedures are tummy tuck and breast augmentation/lift at the same time. However, there can be multiple different combinations.  The answer really depends on you, the exact procedures you had, the type of work you perform and how you heal.  In general, most people can be back at a desk job within 10 days, most gym activities can be restarted within 6 weeks. I do ask that for yoga, no abdominal stretching be done for 8 weeks from the time of surgery.

Can I get a tattoo before my tummy tuck?
Can I get a tattoo before my tummy tuck?
Mar 11, 2020
Assuming you are referring to an abdominal tattoo, it is very common for abdominal tattoos to appear distorted or stretched after a tummy tuck. It would probably be a better idea to wait until after you’ve healed completely and have been cleared from your surgeon before getting a tattoo.  A tattoo on other parts of the body should be okay to have at this time.  Discuss the situation with your Board Certified Plastic Surgeon just to be sure.
Can online school be done during recovery from tummy tuck?
Can online school be done during recovery from tummy tuck?
Mar 11, 2020
It would be very reasonable to say you could resuming online classes if you give yourself 5-7 days off immediately following surgery.  As long as you’re not taking any narcotics, so that you’re mentally sharp to do your schoolwork.  It is important to mobilize and walk around the house while you are recovering in order to avoid the complication of blood clots forming in your legs or pelvis.   Best of Luck!
