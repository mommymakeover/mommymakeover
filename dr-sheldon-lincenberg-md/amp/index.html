Is my nipple grafts failing or infected?
Healing of nipple grafts.
Oct 4, 2018
The way that nipple grafts heal is identical to the way that a skin graft heals. The greater the distance from the underlying bed of nursing, blood vessels, the more likelihood that the skin will die. This is why the areola portion of the nipple graft generally heals without any difficulty, where as the nipple itself oftentimes develops necrosis on its surface. Please be patient and follow the instructions of your surgeon.
1 month post op. Is my nipple graph failing?
Failing nipple graft?
Oct 4, 2018
Thank you for your inquiry. The center of the nipple graft is indeed black and no longer living tissue. This tissue can be gently trimmed away or it will flake off on its own after it dries up. It does not appear to be infected, although the odor is indicative of dead tissue. Most of the time, the healing process will continue and the results that you will have will be very natural and acceptable, albeit with minimal nipple projection.
What is too much bleeding for a (post-op four days) nipple graft?
Postoperative bleeding from nipple grafts.
Oct 4, 2018
It is very common to have some bleeding from around the edges of the nipple graft or the bolster, which sits on top of the graft. Most of the time. This causes unnecessary distress for the patient. The clear plastic bandage allows you to see this small amount of blood, oftentimes exaggerating exactly how much bloodThere is there, and setting up needless worry. Hopefully by the time you read this answer, the situation will be resolved.
