Dr for Botox Eyebrows?
Best Type of Physician for Botox
Jan 22, 2013
Hi Sandra,

You are wise to exercise caution in selecting your physician. Board-certification is a must. Oculoplastic Surgeons (eye surgeons with a sub-specialty in plastic surgery), Plastic Surgeons, and Dermatologists are all excellent choices.

I Have a Few Lines on the Side of My Cheek?
Botox may or may not be for lines in cheek
Jan 20, 2013
If you are not not happy with your dimple, I would suggest considering dermal fillers such as Juviderm, Perlane or Radiesse is that area. Botox can be used for dimples in some circumstances, but must be done very carefully. Your physician would need to assess the location and cause of your dimple and exercise good clinical judgement is determining the best course of action.
Botox Around Eyes and Dilation of Blood Vessels?
Dilation of blood vessels not related to Botox
Jan 20, 2013
It is highly unlikely that the dilation of your vessels is related to the Botox injections. It could be related to other underlying conditions or other systemic issues and suggest that you speak to your primary care physician.
Is Xeomin Being Marketed and Available Yet?
Xeomin is now available in Boston
Jan 20, 2013
Yes, our sales representative has informed us that Xeomin, a botulism toxin is now being marketed to the public as an alternative to Botox and Dysport. I would expect the effects of Xeomin to last the same amount of time as the other brands of botulinum toxin.
What Are the Risks Associated with Cosmetic Use of Botox?
Risks of Botox are minimal
Jan 20, 2013
I have been successfully injecting Botox for over 20 years. In the hands of an experienced physician there are a few risks, but the more common risk is a small bruise at the injection site, which will be experienced by a small percentage of patients.
The Muscles in my Eyelids and Lower Forehead Are Straining to Hold Up my Skin?
Lids feel heavy after Botox
Jan 20, 2013
You describe the onset of the heaviness in your lids occurring recently after a Botox injection, so it is possible there is a correlation. If it was due to the injection, you may need to be patient until the effects of the Botox wears off, but I'm sure you practitioner would appreciate a call from you so he/she can assess the situation.
I Want to Become a Medical Esthetician?
Becoming a medical aesthetician
Jan 14, 2013
In Massachusetts there are several schools that offer advanced training in aesthetics and laser, even though to perform many procedures you should be under the close supervision of a physician. For more advanced procedures, such as Botox, you will need to go to medical school or nursing school.
