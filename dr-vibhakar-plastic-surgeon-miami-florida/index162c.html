Breast Implant rupture but still nursing – how long do I need to wait after nursing to get an ex
Rupture and Breast Feeding
Mar 7, 2023
Hello,You should consider confirming that you have a rupture via either an ultrasound (least invasive) or an MRI (most specific to detect ruptures) and that may help guide your decision about timing. I typically wait a minimum of 6 weeks after breastfeeding to explant depending on severity of pain/symptoms otherwise 3 months is ideal to allow the breast tissue to settle. This should be considered as often a lift is required with the explant.Hope this helps!Dr Dev
Breast Implant Capsules – will they ever dissolve or reabsorb into the body?
They Will NOT dissolve or reabsorb
Mar 7, 2023
Hello. The capsules do not dissolve or reabsorb. Unfortunately, 50% of your capsule will remain in the breast pocket unless it is definitively removed via a Total Capsulectomy.Dr Dev

Hoping to have 14 year old implants explanted under local. Can I get a fat transfer later?
Breast Implant Removal and Fat Transfer
Sep 20, 2020
Good Question. While most patients opt to remove the capsule and implant (which is my personal preference) , yes you can do fat transfer after implant removal only while leaving the capsule behind. You will want to wait 6 months to a year for the skin to ‘shrink’ so you can get an accurate fat transfer for optimal cosmetic outcome. Simple Implant removal can be done under local anesthesia.

Could I still get a mommy makeover while pumping and dumping?
Beast Feeding and Mommy Makeover
Oct 6, 2019
Hi,
You should wait anywhere from 3-6 month after breastfeeding or pumping and dumping prior to doing any surgery. This allows time for your breasts to settle and not be engorged or swollen. Dr. Dev
