Am I too old for a “Mommy Makeover” ?

Don’t worry about age, age is just a number and as long as you are in good health, I think you potentially would be an excellent candidate for elective plastic surgery and mommy makeover.

Congratulations on your weight loss! For patient safety reasons, any patient 50 or older needs a medical clearance from their general doctor as well as recent labs/ekg typically if one hasn’t already been done. I routinely perform mommy makeovers on patients over 50 (many of them over 60) and they do really well.

Make sure to talk to your general doctor and make an appointment with a board certified plastic surgeon.

There are a number of specialty boards and types of board certification, but you – as a consumer – need to understand what is involved with each one.

The American Board of Plastic Surgery is recognized by the American Board of Medical Specialties; the American Board of Cosmetic Surgery is not.

Dr. Tom J. Pousti Endoscopic Tummy Tuck

A surgeon who is board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery has completed at least 3 years of general surgery training and 2-3 years of plastic surgical training.

In addition, to become board certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery, a surgeon has to complete a written and oral examination process as well as continuing medical eduction and recertification for as long as they continue to practice plastic surgery.

These regulations aren’t true for certification by the American Board of Cosmetic Surgery (in some states, a surgeon cannot say that he or she is board certified unless it is a board accredited by the American Board of Medical Specialties).

Other things to look for when choosing your surgeon include making sure that he or she practices in an accredited facility and has hospital privileges to perform the same procedures that you wish to have done. You should also be able to ask to look at pre- and post-operative photos and talk to former patients.

Scar treatment after a Mommy Makeover in Temecula

While there are many options out there to treat scars, I believe less is better. Many of the “treatments” are proven only anecdotally. Silicone is the only thing “proven” to work on scars.

Other treatments can help but can also cause a localized reaction, making the scar worse.

At my practice, I encourage the use of a silicone based gel that also contains an 30 SPF sunscreen. Sun exposure can cause the scars to appear red for a longer period of time.

We start massaging this gel into the incision sites at 3 weeks post-operatively to allow the scar to soften over time. The scar matures over the first year, so be patient and use daily.
