Mommy Makeover in Hollywood can be done in one surgery

Hollywood Mommy Makeover typically refers to surgery to restore and enhance the before-baby figure. This usually involves at least a tummy tuck and breast augmentation with or without a lift.

The 3 procedures of tummy tuck, breast augmentation, and breast lift can be safely performed in one operation. This saves the patient recovery time and is also more cost effective for the patient. The ultimate result would be as good as having 2 or 3 separate procedures.

Surgical drains and tummy tuck after Mommy Makeover surgery

The use of surgical drains after tummy tuck surgery and breast surgery is common. At Gayoso Plastic Surgery, I maintain drains until the drainage amount has reached a particular level.

During this time, the drainage consistency should be changing from thicker, more bloody liquid, to much thinner fluid.

Full Mommy Makeover By Jimmy S. Firouz, MD, FACS

All of these are signs of normal healing and indicate that the drainage is slowing down appropriately.

The risk of removing drains too quickly after surgery is the development of a seroma, which might require aspiration (using a needle) or repeat drain placement.

Therefore, although they can be quite annoying, it is best to remain patient and allow the drains to be in place as long as your body is producing fluid, generaly 5-7 days after surgery.

Preparing for mommy makeover

Your plastic surgeon may have his own specific instructions. I always remind my patients to stay well hydrated, to keep moving in terms of maintaing appropriate blood flow through their legs, and refrain from vigorous activity. Post op weight gain after mommy makeover

The phenomenon you have seen is quite normal in a surgery as extensive as yours. This new “weight” is due to the amount of swelling and fluid retention that has occurred after mommy makeover procedure. As your body recovers from mommy makeover cosmetic surgery, this swelling will decrease and your body will stop retaining fluid.

Your weight should return to your preoperative weight as long as you maintain a healthy lifestyle and diet. This process may take 6 weeks or even as long as a few months in some patients.
