An Abdominoplasty Can Get Rid of Loose Skin From Pregnancy

Mommy makeover is a term assigned to any combination of procedures that treat the unwelcome outcomes of body contour resulting from pregnancy. Typically, after childbearing, a woman may see her breasts small and with more sag. Many times, there is some residual fat in the hip and thigh areas that remain more resistant to diet and exercise.

Often, there is excess skin in the abdomen secondary to the stretching experienced during pregnancy. Some women are lucky enough to have the skin retract sufficiently that no extra skin remains.

Many times, however, there are stretch marks and loose skin in the lower abdomen area. Diet and exercise will burn the underlying fat. Exercise will tone up the muscle, but skin which has been stretched and damaged to the extent that there are stretch marks, will not be eliminated unless it is surgically removed.

The abdominal wall is the area which normally has the excess skin. To a lesser extent, there may be loose skin in the breast area. 

When loose skin is present in the abdomen, the patient will require a tummy tuck for appropriate treatment. If only a small amount of skin is present in the lower portion of the abdomen, then it is possible that the patient may have her preoperative goals achieved with a mini tummy tuck.

A mini tummy tuck will remove loose skin between the pubic bone and the belly button. Normally in this procedure, liposuction is also performed in the abdominal area. If there is loose skin above and below the umbilicus, then the patient is most likely best treated with a full tummy tuck.

In a full tummy tuck, the skin and fat are elevated off of the underlying muscle from the pubic bone up to the rib margin. When lifting it off of the abdominal wall, an incision is made around the belly button to leave it attached to the muscle wall and maintain its normal position.

After the skin and fat layer is loosened, the muscle layer is tightened. The skin and fat are then pulled down as far as possible to remove as much extra skin as possible.

After the skin is removed, a new incision is made so that the underlying belly button can come back out through the skin in its previous position. A full abdominoplasty should be able to treat and remove the vast majority of the extra skin resulting from pregnancy. (John J. Edney, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

Mommy Makeover Gets Rid of Loose, Saggy Skin: Includes Breast Augmentation/Lift, Tummy Tuck & Lipo

Pregnancy has a significant impact on a woman’s body. Obviously, as the abdomen enlarges, the skin becomes stretched and the muscles weakened. After pregnancy, women are left with loose, saggy skin with stretch marks and weakened abdominal muscles.

In addition to this, they frequently have loss of breast volume with breast sag and fat accumulation of the hips and thighs. Many women will attempt diet and exercise to reverse the changes associated with pregnancy.

After multiple pregnancies, this approach is usually unsuccessful. These changes can effect the patient’s self esteem, self confidence, self-image, sexuality, feelings of femininity, and how clothes fit. For most women, the solution for redundant abdominal tissue is removal of the excess skin.

This can be accomplished with an abdominoplasty or mini abdominoplasty, depending on how much loose skin the patient has. These procedures remove excess skin and stretch marks and tighten the abdominal muscles. Mini abdominoplasty is used when the excess skin is located below the umbilicus and avoids detaching the umbilicus from the surrounding tissue. Full abdominoplasty involves totally detaching the belly button and re-inserting it in a higher level after removing the excess skin. Many women who undergo abdominoplasty opt to undergo breast augmentation with breast lift and liposuction of the hips and thighs at the same time. This approach offers synergy, cost savings, and one recovery.

Abdominoplasty and the mommy makeover are both procedures that are associated with extremely high satisfaction rates. (Richard J. Bruneteau, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)
