Kansas Mom Makeover

Mommy Makeover in Kansas is one of the most common procedures performed after weight loss or child birth. The tummy tuck will remove all stretch marks below and at the level of the umbilicus.

Removing the excess skin and suturing the fascia will tighten the abdomen. A combination of breast lift /Implant will address the breast ptosis. Pick a board certified plastic surgeon and do not travel outside the country. If you have a complication, you will end by paying double to fix it.

The term “Mommy Makeover” in Kansas is really just a catch-all for the variety of things that can be done to restore a pre-pregnancy body. That usually is breast and abdominal surgery done simultaneously, maybe with some liposuction.

The reason it usually makes sense to do breast and abdomen together is that the overall recovery is not much longer than it is for abdominal surgery alone.

Body contouring procedures are performed to remove excess skin and fat from the body to produce a more pleasing shape. These procedures can include arm lifts, thigh lifts, butt lifts, liposuction, tummy tucks, and breast lifts.

These procedures are often done in combinations for aesthetic, financial, or convenience reasons. The combination of a breast procedure with an abdominoplasty (and possibly liposuction) is coined a “Mommy Makeover” in Kansas because the goal is to reverse the changes brought about from bearing children and breast-feeding.

The ideal combination, however, is tailored to the needs of each patient and does not have to include the use of implants. See the link below for more info.

Breast Lift Without Implants

Yes, you can get a breast lift without implants – it just depends on what your expectations are after the mommy lift.

After a breast lift, the breast becomes more compact and appears smaller even though no breast tissue is removed except for skin.

I tell my patients to think of liquid in a cup versus liquid spilled all over a countertop. The same volume of fluid appears to be less in a cup when it is contained by the a glass versus when it spilled all over the counter.
