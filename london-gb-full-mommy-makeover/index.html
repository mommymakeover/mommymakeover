London, GB Full Mommy Makeover

Most surgeons in London refer to a “mommy makeover” as a series of cosmetic surgery procedures intended to restore a woman’s figure after pregnancy. I perform these procedures as part of a “mommy makeover” for patients who want an improved figure after having children

Pregnancy, childbirth, and breastfeeding can take a serious toll on a woman’s body, and many moms find that no amount of diet and exercise can restore their pre-baby figures.

Procedures commonly performed during mommy makeovers include, but are not limited to:
– Abominoplasty (tummy tuck)
– Breast Augmentation +/- Lift, or
– Breast Lift only without implants or
– Breast Reduction with lift
– Liposuction

Please visit with a board certified PS to learn more about your options

Medical indications for breast augment and tummy tuck

Insurance has covered a breast augmentation for the chest deformity known as Poland’s Syndrome and to treat the contralateral breast after breast cancer and reconstruction. So insurance coverage is not impossible but only indicated for specific medical diagnosis

Insurance has also covered a panniculectomy, which many people would consider a tummy tuck, when medical problems of the skin under the abdominal skin apron have failed conservative treatments and this has been documented. Breast augment and tummy tuck, done purely for cosmetic purposes, are not covered by any insurance companies

Tummy Tuck And Breast Lift By Dr. Sultan Hassan

A breast augmentation is never covered by insurance. Breast reconstruction with implants after a mastectomy usually is. A tummy tuck might be covered if there is a massive overhan of skin and fat causing symptoms

Breast augmentation would only be considered medically necessary when done as part of a breast reconstruction after breast cancer. Otherwise, tummy tucks and breast augmentations are cosmetic without question. No insurance company would cover the cost

Sleeping on your side shouldn’t be an issue; it’s lying flat that most surgeons do not like. Check with your surgeon.

Sleeping after abdominoplasty and breast surgery is normally best performed on the back to avoid pressure on the implants or the areas of abdominal repair. I usually recommend to my patients that they rent a Lazyboy type chair where they can sleep in a “beach chair” position that allows a mild flex in the waist while still actually being straight back and a slight bend to the knees to maximize blood flow
