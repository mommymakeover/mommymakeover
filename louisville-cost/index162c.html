5 Basic Elements of a Mommy Makeover

Mommy makeover what is it pictures

Louisville Mommy Makeover is a combination of procedures which help post pregnancy females to gain freedom from debilitating insecurities. Pregnancy causes changes in one’s body that most women never anticipated. The joy of children is sometimes unfortunately offset by the disappointment in one’s own body. The Mommy Makeover is a way of giving women back their sense of self confidence, and if done by well trained Board Certified plastic surgeons, are safe and long lasting (often permanent).

The 5 elements are:

  1. tummy tuck (abdominoplasty) in which loose skin is removed, separation of abdominal muscles is corrected permanently, and the belly button is repositioned;
  2. liposuction or body sculpturing of flanks, hips, and sometimes thighs are done at the same time;
  3. breast recontouring to include lifts, implants, and sometimes reductions with liposuction of fat in the anterior armpit known as axillary breasts;
  4. labiaplasty which is a reduction of excess labia which is often a result of elongated labor and, if done correctly, also tightens the vaginal opening;
  5. medical treatment of the mask of pregnancy (dark pigment on any area of the face caused by pregnancy hormones, aka melasma or colasma). (S. Larry Schlesinger, MD, FACS, Honolulu Plastic Surgeon)

How Long Is The Recovery Time For A Mommy Makeover?

The Mommy Makeover is the term that refers to plastic surgical correction of unwanted bodily changes to a woman’s breasts, abdomen, labia, and fat distribution post pregnancy. Recovery time depends on several variables:

  1. how much surgery one has
  2. one’s age and health
  3. the skill of the surgeon and his or her team
  4. one’s support group to include friends, family and extended family

I recently operated on a busy physician, did breast lift with implants, total tummy tuck, medial thigh tuck, and liposuction, and she was back on call at her hospital at 7 days post surgery. On the other hand, I have had patients who took several weeks to return to the simplest chores. In general, I would say a tummy tuck, breast augmentation, liposuction in a 30 year old, motivated mother should take her out of her normal daily activities for about 10 days. My patients normally transition from strong pain killers to ibuprofen at about 5-7 days. One’s age is certainly a factor. I have had a series of 25 year old women who cut hair, and after a tummy tuck and breast augmentation, they are all back at work within 7 days. On the other hand, Mommy Makeover patients in their 60’s tend to take much longer to return to their normal routines. As you can see, the Mommy Makeover recovery time is dependent on multiple factors. The best advice I can give you is to choose a Board Certified plastic surgeon who routinely does Mommy Makeovers, get a good supportive team around you, and allow yourself at least 2-3 weeks to get back into your usual activities. (S. Larry Schlesinger, MD, FACS, Honolulu Plastic Surgeon)
