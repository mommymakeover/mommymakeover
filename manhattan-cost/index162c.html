Mommy Makeover Recovery

The recovery from the tummy tuck will be longer than that from the breast augmentation.

Regarding a tummy tuck, I normally tell my patients that they will need to sleep in a recliner or in bed with pillows under their back and knees for the first week until they are able to completely straighten at the waist; they will need pain medication for the first one to two weeks but the majority of pain will resolve after 3-5 days; no lifting more than 10 lbs or strenuous activity for 6 weeks. For a submuscular breast augmentation, no lifting more than 10 lbs for 4 weeks, pain medication is normally required for 1-3 days but there will be soreness in the muscle for 2-3 weeks.

Your surgeon will provide instructions specific to you and your surgeries. Be sure to follow them. (Nicholas Tarola, MD, Nashville Plastic Surgeon)

How long to recover from a Mommy Makeover

Recovery time from mommy makeover varies depending on the particulars of your procedure and by individual recovery rates. Typically the first 2-3 days you should be up and moving to limit your risks of complications, but taking it easy with no activities to raise your blood pressure (including lifting, exercise, and home stress).

By 2-3 weeks you should be dramatically improved and able to do typically activities, although continue to avoid strenuous activities such as heaving lifting and vigorous exercises. At 6 weeks moms are typically feeling back towards baseline although it will take 3 months-2 years to see your final results. Over time your results often continue to improve.

If you have repair of the abdominal muscles it is important not to lift for a period of approximately 6 weeks. Each mother’s particular treatment and needs are unique. It’s important to seek guidance from your board certified plastic surgeon. (Ryan Diederich, MD, Saint Louis Plastic Surgeon)
