Mom Tummy Tuck In Pennsylvania

What Exactly is a Mommy Makeover?

A mommy makeover in Pennsylvania is a term that was originally used to describe a tummy tuck, a breast lift and breast implant surgery. Now a mommy makeover is used to describe a tummy tuck along with any type of breast procedure.

The goal of a mommy makeover in Pennsylvania is to reverse the changes that occurred during pregnancy so an abdominal procedure such as a tummy tuck is involved along with some type of breast procedure. Some women also choose to add liposuction of the flanks at the same time to gain a smaller waistline along with a flat stomach and perkier breasts.

Mommy makeover considerations

A reasonable plan, based on your photos would be bilateral breast reduction, liposuction of the waist, hips, pubic area and thighs and a tummy tuck.

If you wanted to minimize your ‘down time’ – you could consider liposuction of the abdomen, but this would not result in a flat abdominal contour.

Breast lift, implant size reduction, and tummy tuck can be done together

Absolutely, you can get these operations done together. As for the implant size reduction I find no problem in using a 250 cc implant with a full mastopexy.

Components of a Mommy Makeover

You do not have to have implants with a mommy makeover! There are many variations of the “MM”. Some patients have lost breast volume and want implants.

Other are happy with the amount of breast tissue that they have, and only want them lifted.

Breast Feeding and my Mommy Makeover

I do think that waiting 3-6 months after ceasing is important, as it allows the breasts to return to a more normal state before mommy lift. It would be important for you to check with your EVLT doctor before air travel, to be sure this will not complicate your outcome or increase your risk of problems with blood clots in your legs or lungs.

Equally important would be the concern for these risks around the time of your mommy makeover- so approval of your desired timeline by your EVLT surgeon would be important for this reason as well.

With regard to how best to achieve the necessary communication with your surgeon, I do not think that “virtual” consultations are responsible medicine. Sometimes you can get a reasonable understanding of a patient’s goals and may even be able to offer options for their achievement after reviewing the patient’s appearance in photos (as we sometimes do on this site), but to really understand and make responsible recommendations an in-person visit is necessary.

Multiple procedures

If you are a good candidate for these procedures, are in good health and cost is not a factor, we often combine these procedures to produce beautiful results in our patients.

Fear of anesthesia for Mommy Makeover is common and understandable

The fear of anesthesia for any plastic surgery procedure is very common and understandable. Usually most patients have a greater fear of anesthesia than they do of whatever procedure they are having.

When considering a Mommy Makeover in PA, the traditional fear of anesthesia can be heightened for several reasons. Because you are a mommy and have children, no one wants to have a lethal experience and leave their children minus one caregiver.

Plastic Surgery After Birth With Dr Julio Clavijo-Alvarez

Also because the operation is longer, most mommy makeovers are in the 4 to 5 hour range, the concerns about untoward events happening are greater with the long anesthetic time. These concerns are perfectly natural.

The good news is that the time for this type of plastic surgery is common. The risk of anything adverse happening because of the anesthetic is far less likely than you being in a car accident driving around your neighborhood from a statistlcal standpoint as you are young and healthy. As long as your plastic surgeon is board-certified and the operative facility is properly credentialed, you should do just fine.
