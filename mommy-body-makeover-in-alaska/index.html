Mommy Body Makeover In Alaska

A Mommy makeover in Alaska is a colloquial term and used to indicate a series of procedures aimed to restore a woman’s body back to her pre-pregnancy shape.

Attached below is an article that can supply you with greater information.

A mommy makeover is a combination of surgical procedures performed to restore the mother’s body to its pre-pregnancy shape.

Despite dieting and exercise, women find that loose skin of the abdomen will not tighten and the breast are smaller, deflated and have lost shape.

The surgery typically involves a Tummy Tuck, Liposuction of the hips, flanks, and thighs as well as a Breast Augmentation +/- a Breast Lift.

These procedures are performed in a single or multiple stages depending on the amount of work required.

Patients typically report increased self-esteem, self-image, and an overall improvement in the quality of their lives following mommy tuck.

Please see a board certified plastic surgeon for an in-person examination to discuss the specific details of the procedures. Ask to see before and after photos to get an idea of what can be achieved.

Indications for Mommy Makeover

Generally speaking, Alaska mommy makeover includes a breast augmentation with/without a lift and possibly a tummy tuck. The breast surgery corrects the looseness, sagging, and loss of breast volume that occurs after pregnancy, and the tummy tuck corrects loose, stretched skin of the abdominal wall.

Your breast photos show stretching and sagging of your breasts, as well as loss of volume in the upper cleavage area. My opinion is that a lift would be necessary to reshape your breasts and correct the sagging, and an implant would be helpful to create more fullness in the cleavage area.

Which first: Breast or Abdomen?

The simple answer to this question is “which bothers you more?” if your priority is your abdomen, start with that if not, is not start with the breasts. Most of us would prefer to do both surgeries simultaneously, since it is basically in one field, reducing your “down” time, and even saving you some money.

Doing both does not increase your convalescence, which will be determined by how you react after your abdominoplasty. If you want a quick return to function, the breast operation is less intrusive, and your down time is considerably less.

If you do both together your down time is not cumulative but is the determined by the abdominoplasty. Of course, ask your surgeon how long the both operations will take if performed together. If your surgeon is unable to do both procedures in under five hours, it will be best to separate them.
