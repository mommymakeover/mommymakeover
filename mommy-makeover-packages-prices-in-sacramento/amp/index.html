A “mommy makeover in Sacramento” generally refers to breast and abdominoplasty surgery being done at the same time.

With your dramatic weight loss I imagine that you would benefit from more than just a simple abdominoplasty. Consider a circumferential abdominoplasty that would correct your anterior AND posterior trunk skin excess.

Seek consultation from a board certified plastic surgeon (or two) to learn about your options and to coordinate timing with your continued weight loss.

Timing of Mommy Makeover after breastfeeding

Surgery on the breasts, such as breast augmentation, should not be performed while you are nursing, and should be deferred for at least 3 months after you finish breastfeeding. An in person consultation with a plastic surgeon is a prerequisite to any elective surgery.

Previous childbirth is not a prerequisite for a mommy makeover.

The expression “Sacramento mommy makeover” is colloquial and refers to an unspecified combination of operations to change the aesthetic consequences of pregnancy.

Patients with significant weight loss often have similar aesthetic issues without previous pregnancy. (Vincent N. Zubowicz, MD, Atlanta Plastic Surgeon)

There is a difference between ABCS and ABPS

There is a distinct difference between someone certified by ABCS and ABPS. American Board of Plastic Surgery is one of the specialty boards recognized by the American Board of Medical Specialties. In order to be certified by ABPS the doctor has to undergo specialty training in plastic surgery at an accredited institution usually for three or more years and then undergo a vigorous two part examination based not only on medical knowledge pertinent to plastic surgery but also cases actually performed by the surgeon.

The American Board of Cosmetic Surgery is not recognized by the American Board of Medical Specialties and does not require the same level of specialty training in plastic surgery. Often their training is in fields other than plastic surgery and whatever training they receive in plastic surgery is through courses that are over shorter periods of time, usually days or a couple of weeks. (James Tang, MD, Houston Plastic Surgeon)

Drains after mommy makeover

Surgical drains are typically used with tummy tucks. Most surgeons don’t use them with breast augmentation and liposuction. The tummy tuck drains are usually removed within 1-2 weeks, but the exact timing can depend on how much fluid is draining. (Arnold S. Breitbart, MD, FACS, Long Island Plastic Surgeon)
