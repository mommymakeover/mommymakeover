Tummy tuck breast augmentation surgery

This procedure has been designed to reverse the changes associated with pregnancy. Pregnancy causes loss of breast volume with associated sag, loose, saggy abdominal skin, and fat collections of the hips and thighs.

This combination simultaneously addresses these problems in a safe and cost-effective manner. The procedure is associated with extremely high satisfaction rates.

After gastric bypass, many patients are not candidates for mini-abdominoplasty because of the amount of loose skin that is present. These patients will frequently require a full abdominoplasty. In addition, loss of breast volume may be associated with breast sag. These patients will often require combination breast lift and augmentation to accomplish their goals. Every patient is unique and with appropriate evaluation and treatment, the mommy makeover in Dayton can successfully address problems that are associated with pregnancy. (Richard J. This would be a “mini-mommy makeover plus.”

As patients (and their doctors) use clever terminology to describe surgical procedures, this breast and (mini)-tummy tuck surgery plus liposuction could be called a “mini-mommy makeover plus!” Of course, the breast surgery could be implants PLUS breast lift, and the abdominal surgery a ful tummy tuck, and this could be called the “perky full mommy makeover.”

Add lipo and you have the “perky full mommy makeover plus.” Let’s just stop the foolishness of simplifying for sake of ease, and use proper terms for sake of clarity! You can have any combination of procedures you and your surgeon deem appropriate and reasonable, so long as proper safety considerations are met, which is the job of every experienced, board-certified (American Board of Plastic Surgery) plastic surgeon who performs these operations separately or together.

Of course, multiple procedures at one time are safe and efficient use of precious time off work, vacation, and everyday activities, and cost is also a consideration. BUT, nothing should trump safety. Cost will vary with surgeon, location in the country, hospital, outpatient surgical facility, or accredited office surgical facility. Hospital care is usually the most costly, and you have the potential exposure to sick patients and hospital bacteria (not to mention a somewhat higher nausea and vomiting rate, as a result of inhalation anesthesia–this can cause an unplanned overnight admission, and even more cost.) Many plastic surgeons utilize free-standing, hospital-affiliated outpatient surgical facilities.

Since these are often staffed by hospital personnel, many of the same concerns (anesthesia and cost) exist, but since these centers are geared to outpatient surgery–like many plastic surgical procedures–there is higher likelihood of a smooth procedure and recovery, and somewhat lower cost. Few plastic surgeons have busy enough cosmetic surgery practices to support their own accredited in-office surgical facilities. But when they do, these tend to be staffed by plastic surgery-dedicated nurses, anesthesia providers, and the plastic surgeon’s own hand-picked and experienced staff. There is no overcharging to cover unreimbursed costs (hospitals that have to care for patients without insurance, for example), and there are no sick patients or nasty hospital bacteria. Costs are carefully controlled, and efficiency, confidentiality, and quality of care are usually top-notch. (You can guess which facility I operate at, though I have full plastic surgical privileges and credentials at several hospitals). Some “cosmetic surgeons” operate in their own facilities to avoid peer review or credentials committees, so make sure your plastic surgeon is American Board of Plastic Surgery certified, and the facility is AAAASF-certified. (Richard H. Tholen, MD, FACS, Los Angeles Plastic Surgeon)
