Plastic Surgery After Birth In Plano, TX

A “mommy makeover in Plano” isn’t a specific procedure or procedures, it is term used for women who wish to restore their figure following childbearing.

Most often this includes a tummy tuck, breast augmentation, breast lift or breast reduction, and sometimes liposuction or other restorative procedures.

Childbearing can take its toll on the body, and many women find that by combining cosmetic procedures they are able to restore their figure more quickly and save on surgical facility fees and downtime.

The same procedures can be performed independently. Please see a board certified plastic surgeon with extensive experience in breast and body contouring for optimal results.

Is it possible to get tummy tuck and breast augmentation at the same time?

Yes, both procedures can be done at the same time, this is sometimes called a “Plano TX mommy makeover”. Be sure your surgeon is experienced as this takes longer than just one procedure at a time.

Scar treatment after mommy makeover

I treat many patients who have had tummy tucks by others and want to improve results. A combination of pulsed dye laser and Melarase cream can improve the scar texture and color.

We advise patients to use the Plato’s Scar Serum to help with hypertrophic scars.

Tummy tuck and breast augmentation at surgery center.

Actually you are probably at lower risk for infection and other problems at the surgery center versus the hospital.

If the surgery center is accredited and your plastic surgeon board certified, I wouldn’t worry.

Combining cosmetic plastic surgery procedures like a tummy tuck and breast augmentation is not uncommon. Whether you are a reasonable candidate for those procedures depends on your anatomy, your medical history, the extent of the procedures, realistic expectations of the outcome and an understanding of the pros and cons of the various procedures.

Dr Ken Smart Mini Mommy Makeover

Safety should always be the main concern. It is generally recommended that the length of elective surgery be less than six hours. The procedures should be performed in an ambulatory facility that is accredited by one of the national organizations like the American Association for Accreditation of Ambulatory Surgical Facilities (AAAASF) or in a licensed hospital.

The safety of procedures performed in a AAAASF surgical facility have been well documented in the medical literature. No matter where the procedures are performed, many surgeons would recommend an overnight stay at an appropriate facility where you can be appropriately monitored.

Keep in mind that following the advice of any surgeon on this or any other web site who proposes to tell you what to do without: examining you, physically feeling the tissue, assessing your desired outcome, and taking a full medical history, as well as discussing the pros and cons of each operative option would not be in your best interest.

Board certification for plastic surgeons

Unfortunately, someone boarded by the ABCS is generally an ear nose and throat surgeon not a plastic and reconstructive surgeon. Training for plastics is very specific and only surgeons with specific training in plastic and reconstructive surgery are boarded by the American Board of Plastic Surgeons.

I would strongly recommend you choose a surgeon with ABPS credentials for your Mommy Makeover surgery

ABCS is not a recognized board unlike the American board of plastic surgery which is the only certification for true plastic surgeons. You will probably notice that your surgeon is not on realself because his qualifications are not adequate to even be listed.

Check closely and I suspect you will find that your surgeon is actually an oral surgeon or ear nose and throat doctor, or possibly has no formal training. None of the titles will be plastic surgeon and none of them would be able to do a mommy makeover in a hospital.

This is a quick test that’s easy to do. Only a real plastic surgeon will have actual hospital privileges to do cosmetic procedures. A lesser surgeon won’t have privileges because their training is not recognized and their certification is unacceptable for hospital credentialing committees. Beware.

Too old for “mommy makeover”?
