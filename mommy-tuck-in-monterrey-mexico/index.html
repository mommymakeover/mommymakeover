Mommy Tuck In Monterrey, Mexico

A mommy makeover in Monterrey is usually for a woman who has had children and wants to regain her prepregnancy body back. It will usually consist of a tummy tuck and an operation for the breasts (breast lift and or augmentation)

Mommy makeover in one operation

We do the combination of breast surgery and abdominoplasty on a regular basis. The advantages are one recovery time and cost savings by combining them. The safety of the procedure has been established as long as the surgery time isn’t too long.

Breast surgery and abdominoplasty – mommy makeover

It is not uncommon to combine breast surgery and abdominoplasty. In your case the required abdominal surgery is so minimal due to your healthy lifestyle that adding the breast surgery at the same time is much safer that it might otherwise be.

Doing an abdominoplasty with breast surgery is a very common combination. This is done together very frequently without trouble. As long as you are healthy to undergo the mommy surgery which should take about 5 hours, you should do very well.

The down time will be about 2 weeks but you will still be recovering for about 6 weeks. That means that after two weeks you will feel much better but it will take about 6 weeks to get back to normal activity such as gym workouts.

Safe to combine if you are in good health

I can assume from you photo your BMI (Body Mass Index) is below 30 which makes it safe for you to combine procedures assuming you have no other medical issues.

Mommy Lift Is Designed To Counteract The Physical Effects Of Childbearing

The combination of the procedures you are interested in are combined safely all the time. This allows you one anesthesia and only one recovery time which is also beneficial for you.

Tummy tuck with breast augmentation is a safe combination

Though the recovery can be more challenging, it is safe to combine a tummy tuck with breast augmentation, and keep the time in mommy lifting within the four to five hour window. Long periods of time in mommy tuck can increase the infection risk, and the risk of blood clots which can form in your legs. If you choose to go for both, plan ahead at home as you will be sore top and bottom.

Also ask to have a pair of surgical compression stockings and a leg massager (SCD’s) during your procedure, and be prepared to get up and move right after to reduce your risks

Multiple variables will affect the safety of combination procedures in Plastic Surgery

Dr. Gabriel Angel Mecott Rivera Mommy Makeover

What might be safe under certain circumstances may not be under others… And remember – not all things that can be done should be done…

Important variables to consider would include your surgeon (education, training, experience, reputation, outcomes, integrity), the facility, the anesthesia provider, and (very significantly) the length of the proposed operation…

Some surgeons may be able to do the procedure for you in 4 hours (more safe), while others may need a lot more time to complete the procedure (less safe). Generally speaking, I would not consider an operation longer than 4-5 hours to be safe unless the patient were to spend the night in a facility for postoperative monitoring

Multiple procedures: proceed with caution

It is certainly possible to do all 3 procedures under one anesthetic, but certain risks increase with longer operations. I would be more worried about blood clots in the legs forming, which can travel to the lungs and be very serious
