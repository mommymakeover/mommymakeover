The Mommy Make-Over in South Dakota is associated with extremely high satisfaction rates. The procedure often involves breast augmentation, breast lift, liposuction, and abdominoplasty.

It would appear from reviewing your pictures that you’re an excellent candidate for an abdominoplasty. In the mid-western United States where I practice, a cost of $11,000 for a tummy tuck and saline breast augmentation would not be considered unreasonable.

It’s safe to say that it’s impossible to perform a tummy tuck without scarring. This operation trades stretch marks, loose skin, and weakened muscles for a scar that is hopefully well positioned.

The patient’s particular anatomy will have a profound impact on scar placement. Factors such as the transverse width of the abdomen, the distance from the pubis to the rib cage, the position of the umbilicus, and the patient’s stretch marks are all important variables.

The placement of the lower abdominal skin incision is of critical importance when performing abdominoplasty.

Most women want a low incision so it will be hidden when they wear their swimsuit. From reviewing your pictures, it would seem likely that this would be possible for you.

Each patient is unique and it’s essential to have good communication between the patient and the surgeon. I find it very helpful to perform pre-operative markings with the patient wearing her swimsuit.

By utilizing this maneuver, confusion regarding incision placement is minimized. With normal wound healing and a well placed incision, most patients are back in their bikinis twelve months after mommy makeover in South Dakota.
