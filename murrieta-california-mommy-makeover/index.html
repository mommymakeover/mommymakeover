Loose skin from pregnancy

The “Mommy Makeover” is the term used to describe a tummy tuck and/or breast lift after pregnancy. For many women after pregnancy, despite diet and exercise, the rapid growth of the abdomen and breasts is more than their skin can handle.

This can leave stretch marks and loose skin. The only effective treatments for stretch marks and loose skin of the abdomen is a tummy tuck where this skin is removed.

For the breasts, either a lift or a lift with an implant may be necessary to improve the appearance. In a breast lift, the excess skin is removed but the breast tissue is reshaped to create the perky pre-pregnancy breast.

An implant is added if more volume is needed than what remains after pregnancy. Considerations before having these procedures are whether you plan on having any more children, implications for breast cancer and breast cancer reconstruction (particularly if breast cancer runs in the family), and staging of surgical procedures as the complication rates of surgery and anesthesia increase significantly when combining breast and abdominal procedures.

Be sure to address all of these issues with your Plastic Surgeon, and make sure they are board certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery.
