Plastic Surgery After Baby In Tucson, AZ

The ideal goal of the Mommy Makeover in Tucson, AZ is to reverse the physical changes that accompany pregnancy, breast feeding and the resulting changes that they produce on your body. So, it may vary from one individual to another but typical procedures include tummy tuck, liposuction, breast lift with or without implants and for some breast reduction.

Just what you will need is up to you to first determine your priority zones and then converse with your (Board Certified I hope!) plastic surgeon. Be certain to request that you get to see photos of your surgeon’s work.

Outpatient mommy makeover is safe

Assuming your plastic surgeon is board certified and the procedure is taking place at an accredited facility, there is no reason why a healthy patient cannot have it done an an outpatient facility. Many plastic surgeons have an accredited surgical facility with their office.

BUYER BEWARE!!! ONLY GO WITH A BOARD CERTIFIED PLASTIC SURGEON FOR COSMETIC SURGERY

Please do your homework! The American Board of Plastic Surgery requires rigorous training and testing for all of their surgeons. The “other” board pretty much lets anyone into their “club” without the same training as a board certified plastic surgeon!

ABCS is not a real board

No, ABCS is not the same as ABPS. Check what certification your doctor has and you will probably find out that he/she is NOT a board certified plastic surgeon but rather an Ear/Nose/Throat doctor or general surgeon or something else.

The American Board of Plastic Surgery is the only board certification for Plastic Surgeons which has very high requirements for the surgeon. Be careful!

Is it possible to get tummy tuck and breast augmentation at the same time?

If you are in excellent health and a good candidate for breast augmentation and tummy tuck, then yes you can have both procedures at the same surgery. WE often call this a Tucson, AZ Mommy Makeover.

Combining Multiple Appropriate Procedures In Tucson AZ

Scar therapy

Use the scarguard you have for the first several months. At four weeks you can start to massage your incisions – lubricate your finger with lotion or oil (anything you prefer is fine) and put firm gentle pressure along all of your scars. Do this several times a day for the first year. Also avoid any sun on your scars.

With regard to timing of mommy tuck relative to breast feeding, I would recommend waiting at least 6 months after completion of breast feeding, however, this is a loose number and a few visits with your surgeon would be required to ensure optimal timing.

Having said that, I would discourage you from travel from NY to FL for mommy lift. You are not looking to get a mole removed or a quick little office procedure. The surgeries you are looking to have done are great, with a high satisfaction rate – however, they require multiple post-operative visits in best case scenarios.

Anesthesia is safe if you’re healthy and your anesthesia provider is qualified.

You are young and healthy, so anesthesia concerns are truly minimal, even with long procedures.

Dr Jennifer Boll Tummy Tuck And Breast Augmentation Recovery Photos Result

Your surgeon should be a board-certified (American Board of Plastic Surgery) plastic surgeon, the facility accredited (especially if it is an office-based facility), and the anesthesia provider should be a board-certified anesthesiologist (American Board of Anesthesia) who actually stays in the operating room for the entire procedure, not just supervises a nurse anesthetist (CRNA) from another room, or a qualified, experienced, and properly-trained, licensed CRNA who actually stays with you and monitors your vital signs as anesthesia is administered.

Sad to say, all too often in a hospital setting or hospital personnel-staffed outpatient surgical center, an elective procedure on a healthy, young patient like you will be considered an “easy anesthetic” and you will meet the anesthesia doctor and his “team,” which is read-between-the lines for the CRNA that actually administers the anesthesia while supervised by the anesthesiologist who is no longer in your operating room.

You are anesthetized, the breathing tube placed, everything checked, and then the anesthesiologist leaves! (Perhaps to another operating room so more than one procedure can be “supervised” and billed-for at a time, or perhaps to an operating room where there is a sicker, older, or more critical patient.)

Dr Remus Repta The Mommy Makeover

I trained and have worked at hospitals for 30 years and can promise you that this is not a misrepresentation. In our AAAASF-accredited office surgical facility we made the conscious choice to utilize the highest-quality CRNA providers we can find, since they are actually in the room with the patient, have the day-to-day experience in actually caring for patients, and aren’t “second guessed’ by a physician who is not in the room, hasn’t seen the monitors in real time, and hasn’t been with the patient since the beginning of the operation.

Please understand that I’m not “knocking” my anesthesia colleagues who actually stay in the rooms and monitor their patients in real time while adjusting the anesthetic drugs appropriately;

I just don’t like the idea of charging you for services that you don’t receive, and “supervision” is overrated. Our CRNAs have performed over 16,000 anesthetics in our facility over the past 19 years without death, heart attack, or stroke.

How Do I Prepare For A Mommy Makeover In Tucson

For a young healthy patient such as you, any qualified facility and surgical/anesthesia team can give you a safe experience. Just make sure you know who is actually in the room for your entire surgery.

Rest assured your care will also involve soft padding on the OR table, pneumatic boots to keep the blood flowing in your legs to minimize the risks of blood clots, and a full set of monitors that check your pulse, EKG, oxygen and CO2 levels, blood pressure, and other vital signs such as temperature and fluids (in and out). Every surgery has risks; everything should be directed at minimizing those risks.

We routinely perform multiple-procedure operations that last 6, 7, and up to 8 hours, and still allow outpatient care. This is due not to my skill or talent, but to the skill and techniques of my anesthesia staff.
