Mommy makeover – surgical drains

Surgical drains help to prevent the accumulation of fluid within the abdominal tissues.

They involve small bulbs that provide constant suction to allow the evacuation of fluid. When the amount decreases to a beneath a certain threshold, then you can remove the drains.

Most drains stay in for 1 week, but it is not uncommon to keep them in longer if there is still quite a bit of fluid accumulation.

The risk of taking drains out prematurely is that you can develop a seroma which may require drainage or aspiration if it gets to big. The best way to treat a seroma is prevention.

Avoid salt, eat more protein than carbs, and walk as much as your surgeon allows. It usually takes about a month to pee it all off.

I see this with lots of my patients after a mommy makeover or any body contouring procedure. The reason is due to water retention. The response to mommy tuck is inflammation which triggers your body to reabsorb water into the cells. Therefore one will gain weight post operatively and can be several pounds.
