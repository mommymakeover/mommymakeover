Cosmetic Surgery After Pregnancy Price In Canada

A mommy make-over in Canada is a term used for any tummy tuck combined with any breast surgery. What is needed and the cost depends on what you need and want done

You can have a mommy make-over after a mesh repair of an umbilical hernia. Always see a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon.

Most times a mommy makeover involves a tummy tuck, breast augmentation/lift and liposuction. This would be determined with your overall goals. Seek a consult with a board certified plastic surgeon to discuss your goals

Mini Tummy Tuck with Breast Lift

Patients will commonly combine procedures like a Tummy Tuck and a Breast Lift procedure. The advantage is you have only one mommy lift in Canada with anesthesia and one recovery time with possibly some financial benefit.

The recovery should be smooth as both of these procedures do not involve muscle repair or stretching which causes more discomfort than skin incisions

Tummy tucks and breast lifts are commonly performed at the same time. It is very safe to have these operations performed together. Most women find that the breast lift operation is actually much less painful than they are expecting

These procedures are commonly done with one trip to the operating room. Surgeons evaluate each patient based on medical history and other factors to determine if she is a good candidate for the surgeries considered

The recovery from these two procedures together will not be significantly different than recovering from one alone.

Sleeping on your Side after Tummy Tuck Surgery?

Lots of patients suffer from back pain due to the fact that they are sleeping on their backs. Generally, I would say that sleeping on your side (in the fetal position) is fine and will probably help the back pain but I would ask that you ask your surgeon and follow his/her instructions regarding sleeping on your side

Consult with your Plastic Surgeon for his instructions. I see no reason why you cannot sleep on your side. Be sure to cushion yourself with pillows for the most comfort. Always wear your binder until your surgeon tells you otherwise

Many patients want to avoid muscle repair because of concerns for more pain and longer healing. In most situations, putting in this extra effort goes far to giving the patient the flattest belly possible. I think most patients would be very disappointed with going through such a large mommy makeup, and six weeks later, when you feel mostly healed, you still has significant fullness

Muscle repair and tummy tuck

I would strongly suggest having the muscle plication (repair) during your tummy tuck if you want the flattest possible contour. The fact that you have had two pregnancies is a sign that your muscle has been weakened to some degree

Tummy tuck and muscle tightening

In my opinion, muscle tightening (plication) is and should be an integral part of a tummy tuck (abdominoplasty) for optimal results.

You may want to consider a consultation with a different board certified Plastic Surgeon. From the photos and your description, you are an ideal candidate for muscle tightening. While the recovery will be easier for you, your result will likely be suboptimal

Muscle separation

I’ve never really done a tummy tuck without a muscle repair. Any degree of separation is an indication for plication of the abdominal wall. I would suggest you aren’t getting the best result unless this is done
