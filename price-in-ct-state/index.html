Mommy Maker Price In Connecticut

The term of “mommy makeover” in Connecticut is used to name a group of procedures that usually can be done together like:
– Lipoesculpture
– Brazilian butt lift (BBL)
– Breast treatment (Augmentation, reduction or lift)
– Tummy tuck or mini tummy tuck

Combining procedures is very common in Plastic Surgery, this is what we call MMO (mommy makeover). Having the combined procedures done a the same time depends on your doctor. You seem to be a good candidate for both procedures.

A breast lift and a tummy tuck are frequently combined into one mommy surgery. From your photos you would do great with a lift and no implants since you have adequate breaset volume.

Implants do not necessarily need to be redone unless you are having issues. I’m surprised your surgeon didn’t cover this with you, but all pressing type exercises like push ups and bench press will cause the pec muscles to push submuscular implants down and out.

While this isn’t necessarily dangerous, over time it will cause stretching of the lower outer capsule, and the implants will drift out that way.

I tell my patients that they made a trade off – beautiful breasts for bulky pecs.

Do some toning exercise for your pecs, but that’s it. Your breasts will look more natural and retain their position longer.

If you wish to tone your arms, there are plenty of alternative exercises that you can do for that. You can even consult a trainer if you need help coming up with a new regimen.

Combination Of Plastic Surgery Procedures Designed To Address The Breasts, Abdomen, Or Other Parts Of The Body

It looks like you will need a tummy tuck/lipo and breast lift or breast reduction depending on what size you want to be. Your results will depend on your anatomy, your height and weight, and of course the surgeon’s skill.

It would be best for you to seek out consultations with board certified plastic surgeons who can examine you and go over your options.

I would need to see you in the office to really assess you, but based on these photos I would likely recommend a tummy tuck, liposuction and then either a small reduction, breast lift, or lift with augmentation depending on what size of breasts you wanted. A lot depends on what you want, what you are looking for, and what your overall health is.

It’s actually a common thing to combine these procedures. As long as you’re a healthy patient who finds a surgeon that is good on each procedure and feels comfortable with performing the operation, you’re good to go.
