All about Mommy Makeovers

A Mommy Makeover in Omaha NE can be lots of different combinations of treatments. As you can tell from the name, the goal is to help improve the changes that came along with having children.

That beautiful bouncing bundle of joy may have caused parts of your body to stop bouncing and others to start when they never did before.

But fear not, there is a long list of possible procedures to improve most unwanted changes that came along with the very wanted “Junior”. The usual combination deals with the tummy and breasts.

The tummy is usually improved with a tummy tuck (abdominoplasty) and/or liposuction. A tummy tuck is good for removing excess loose and hanging skin and the fat that goes along with it. Lipo can improve areas of fat where skin is not being removed (for example, the upper abdomen).

Breasts can be improved with augmentation (implants), a breast lift, or a combination (most often the best choice). While these procedures can be done in stages, in my opinion one stage is the best way to go if it can be done safely.

This will depend on the amount of work needed and the time necessary to get it done in the operating room. OR time starts at around 3 hours and goes up from there.

Omaha mommy makeover lasting 8 or more hours can be done safely, but should be limited to very healthy patients.

The good news is that most patients can get everything done in one visit to the operating room.

Why Is The Procedure Called A Mommy Makeover

I would recommend that you visit with a few board certified plastic surgeons and get their opinions. He/she can explain your issues and choices for improvement. You can then form a good plan together and begin on the road to the new you.

Preparing for Mommy Makeover

There are many different types of surgeries involved in a mommy makeover in Omaha, and your pre-operative preparation may vary based on which ones you will be undergoing. In general, you are best advised to avoid smoking, eat a well balanced diet and discontinue most medications such as anti-inflammatory pain medication and herbal supplements.

Your plastic surgeon should provide you with a detailed list of his or her recommendations fro both before and after your mommy lift.

How many surgeries to do my mommy makeover?

Mommies don’t get much time off. In general, it is good to group surgeries so you can have one anesthetic and one recovery period, as long as it is safe. The procedure you discussed is one I just did as one surgery – breast lift with implant and tummy tuck – and she did great. But a blanket statement “do one surgery for the mommy makeover” is not accurate as well.

Do you have medical problems? Anemia? Diabetes? Smoker? (in which case I wouldn’t do the lift or the tummy tuck until you stopped.)

When can I resume normal activities after Mommy Makeover?

Light activity should be possible for you according to your comfort within a couple of weeks after the surgery, but I usually prefer that my patients not engage in more strenuous activities, such as heavy exercise, yard work, or heavy housekeeping for 6 weeks.

It is important that you ask your surgeon this question, though, as only he or she will know the details of your surgery and what works best to give his/her patients the best outcomes.

Weight Gain Days After mommy lift plastic surgery

Mom Plastic Surgery Package Is Specifically Designed For Your Unique Needs

Weight gain days after mommy plastic surgery package is common and most often due to swelling within the tissues (edema) and fluid given during surgery (through the IV and, if liposuction was performed, from the fluid injected into tissues to minimize blood loss and discomfort). This fluid will be mobilized with time and your body will excrete it out through the urine.

You would not be the first patient to weigh themselves shortly after surgery and find that their weight has increased! Even if your procedure involved removal of fat with liposuction, breast tissue with a breast reductions or skin with a tummy tuck.

This increased weight is primarily from the extra fluids given to you through the drip during mommy makeover and in the initial recovery phase after the procedure.

The bodies natural response to mom plastic surgery is also to retain fluid, the kidneys retain salt and water to aid the bodies recovery.

Although you will begin to offload the accumulated fluid within a few days of the procedure it may take several weeks and up to a month to return to your preoperative weight. Don’t be too concerned, you will loose that extra weight!
