Mommy makeovers in the District of Columbia differ between patients

The term “mommy makeover” refers to any combination of procedures that improves the changes that occur with pregnancy.

Some patients may need a tummy tuck, liposuction anywhere, breast augmentation, breast lift or reduction, etc.

Components of these procedures include reshaping the belly button and the nipple-areolar complexes.

Have a consultation with an ABPS certified plastic surgeon to find a combination that suits you.

Combined procedures

Generally speaking if the total of your procedures can be done in less than six hours, it’s safe to have them combined. You must be in good overall health.

The risk of longer, combined procedures is that of a higher incidence of a blood clot forming in your legs. Discuss this with your plastic surgeon at the time of the consultation for more detailed information

Combined procedures can be safe in the right candidates. If you are in good health and your surgeon is efficient in performing the procedures it can be safe.

Prolonged mommy lift is associated with a slightly higher risk of getting cold, and possibly blood clots. These risks can be managed but a careful plan is mandatory. Discuss these matters further with your surgeon.

In general swimming in a lake, community pool or ocean is allowed once the incisions are well healed. Some patients are given the all clear by 3 weeks but if there are any slower to heal areas you should seek clearance pror to swimming. This could help to avoid an infection.

Swimming or Jacuzzi after a tummy tuck and breast lift surgery

As you can see from the many responses that your question has received, there is no one answer or universal rule regarding swimming and jacuzzi’s post-operatively. Each surgeon has their own specific philosophy and post-op protocols/restrictions, so it would be advisable that you follow up with your operating surgeon and follow their recommendation.

In my practice, I request that patients refrain from all exercising, swimming included, for at least 4 weeks after mommy tuck in the District of Columbia. I also ask that patients avoid submerging their incisions in swimming pools and jacuzzi’s for at least 4-6 weeks to avoid the possibility of any post-op complication such as infection from contaminants, thermal burns and increased swelling from the warm/hot water.

In general, you would want all wounds to be completely healed and sealed before any swimming or jacuzzi. Check with your surgeon postoperatively.
