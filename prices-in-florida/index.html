Mommy Makeovers In Florida are routinely performed at a single stage. The operation in total should not be more than 6 hours, and it is very safe to perform all 3 at the same time provided they are done in an accredited facility staffed by Board-Certified Anesthesiologists, and by a Board-Certified Plastic Surgeon.

As far as outcome goes, there is definitely more predictability in separating the breast augmentation and breast lift into two stages.

However, it still is routinely done at one stage, and most often without any issues.

Six weeks should be an appropriate amount of time, but this really depends on the comfort of your surgeon.

A mommy makeover procedure typically involves a tummy tuck and breast surgery performed at the same time.

Sometimes this will be a tummy tuck combined with a breast lift/implant surgery. The ideal sequence depends on the recommendations of your surgeon.

Mommy Makeover Preparation

I will generally tell patients who are getting ready for or considering a mommy makeover in Florida to get as close to their ideal weight as possible.

As the surgery draws near, there is really very little to do other than follow your surgeon instructions for the mom makeover.

Usually that includes items like stop smoking (anything) and staying away from second hand smoke. In addition, you should avoid herbals and medications that increase bleeding, like ibuprofen and aspirin.

A Sagging Or Drooping Appearance In The Breasts Due To Excess Skin And Fat Deposits

During mommy makeover all patients are given some intravenous fluid. Additionally, tummy tuck and liposuction usually involve injecting fairly high volumes of fluid to help breakup fat, reduce bleeding, and reduce pain after surgery (infiltration).

The mommy lift in Florida causes your body to retain fluids, including the extra fluids from the intravenous and infiltration. This is called “third space” fluid because it is outside of the cells and other blood stream.

The fluid can be several kilograms! Your body starts to mobilise this fluid after 3 days from mommy surgery and you will be going to the bathroom often because of this.

It usually takes your body about 4 weeks to get rid of 80% of the fluid / swelling, but the residual fluid can take 3-6 months. Your weight will come down over that time provided you maintain a healthy lifestyle!

Drains after mommy makeover

I tell my abdominoplasty patients to expect to have their drains in place for at least a week.

Typically the decision to remove drains is not dogmatic and based on an arbitrary time schedule, rather it is based upon how much fluid is draining out of them.

After Tummy Tuck In Orlando FL

Classically we like to see 30 cc or less per drain per 24 hours before removal. Sometimes liposuction with tummy tuck can increase the drainage and hence the time that the drains will stay in place.

I typically do not use drains. However, when I do, they are usually in for one to two weeks depending on the drain output after mom plastic surgery package.

The tummy tuck and the breast surgery should be at the same time.  The lift and augmentation should be separate by 6 months.

Weight gain after mommy tuck surgery

Excess Fat And Skin In The Abdomen

Weight gain is quite normal and usually temporary. The physiology behind post operative weight gain is secondary to:

  1. fluid retention– with any surgery there is some tissue damage and the body reacts by “hanging on to fluid” also called edema. Like when you sprain your ankle the area swells and as the tissue heals so does the ankle decrease in size. In abdominoplasty ( tummy tuck) there is a considerable amount of tissue resected, moved , streched and surgically manipulated. Thus, increased fluid retention.
  2. with any physical trauma or surgery the body automatically release hormones that enhance fluid retention.
  3. inflammation which is the body’s normal localized response also increases fluid retention.
  4. continued trauma, infection, dependent positioning and immediate heat will only increase the swelling not help. Thus, like you see with athletes elevate the area, apply ice, take some antiinflamatories and usually the majority of the swelling will resolve in 2-3 week with all swelling be gone in several months
