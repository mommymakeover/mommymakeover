Mommy Makeover in Massachusetts is a generic term we use to describe a combination of procedures that are done to restore a woman’s pre-pregnancy shape.

Usually this involves a Tummy Tuck, although it may be just liposuction of the abdomen or a combination of the two. Also some type of breast operation, either a lift, reduction or augmentation.

Bascally, whatever concerns you can be addressed. I have even done labiaplasty as part of the procedure. The most important thing is not to try to combine too many procedures at once. I try to keep the OR time to a 4 hour max if we plan on doing an outpatient procedure, anything longer usually requires an overnight stay.

Just a few years ago, the answer would be: no, that’s too much surgery. But times have changed and if the surgeon has a good support staff, assistant in mommy lift, your health is fine (special attention to a history of blood clots in the legs should be paid), and the lipo volume is not expected to be over 2500 cc’s or so, the plan to do it all at once isn’t crazy.

On some larger cases, I bring in an assistant surgeon to move things along so the breasts and abdomen can be sutured closed at the same time. The whole goal is to keep surgery time to below 6 hours.

Longer times in the OR are associated with complications. I only operate in accredited surgery centers with plenty of support, and every patient understands that if at any point it is unwise to proceed, we will abbreviate the surgery. So if all of this planning has been done, and you are healthy, the answer is that yes, it’s safe.
