Rhode Island Mommy Tuck Prices

Rhode Island Mommy makeover refers to plastic surgical procedures commonly requested by women after pregnancy. This is not a medical term and there is no standard combination of operations.

Most commonly, patients are interested in breast (breast augmentation, breast lifting, breast reduction), abdominoplasty and/or liposuction surgery.

Patients should only address those “problem areas” the concern them enough to justify the risks and potential complications associated with the procedures available to treat the problem area.

Remember, you are in the drivers seat–you can choose to do any portion (if any of) of the “recommended procedure”.

Definition of mommy makoever

There is no one standard procedure that constitutes a mommy makoever–but common interpretation is a combination of breast, tummy, and body procedures on parts of the woman’ anatomy affected by pregnancy.

The breast can be implant, lift, lift with implant, or even reduction. Tummy procedures can be tummy tuck, liposuction or a combination. Body procedures can be liposuction or fat transfers.

Most important thing is to customize your goals.

How A Mommy Makeover In RI Can Help

How soon can I get back in the gym for a mild workout after a mommy makeover?

I would recommend that you discuss this question with your surgeon. Every surgeon has their own specific post op protocol for their patients and your surgeon is the best resource when questions arise.

They are most familiar with your medical history, details of your RI Mommy Makeover, and how you are healing. In my office, I recommend that my patients avoid lifting greater than 10 pounds for the first six weeks. This gives the muscle repair time to heal. They can start gentle cardio at four weeks, but avoid chest exercises for six weeks also.

Dr Leon Goldstein Mommy Plastic Surgery Package Images Results

Working out after mommy surgery

It depends a bit on what exactly you had done during your mommy tuck. I think a general rule of thumb is that you can do some light walking in the first week even. Then slowly progress in terms of light cardio.

You will want to avoid straining if you had abdominal muscle repair (plication) for 4-6 weeks. I do let my patients do very light weight training of their arms within the first month if they are careful, but they need to avoid chest workouts if implants were placed. I’d check w/ your surgeon as they will give you the most direct advice for your case.

Most important – your surgeon has to be you final guide so always check with her/him. On average, mommy make-over recovery goes like this -At two weeks, maybe short walks a few times a week.

At four weeks, more energy, one can start doing light weight, longer walking, gentle swimming. At 6-8 weeks, aerobic exercise (jumping, biking, fast lap swimming, sports.)

Always start back slowly – I have my patients start at 10% of what they did before Rhode Island mommy makeover, e.g. if they walked an hour, they start with a 6 minute walk and work up.

Your body is an excellent guide – if it doesn’t feel right to do, stop doing it. Always see a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon.
