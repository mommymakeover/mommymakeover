San Antonio Plastic Surgery Mommy Makeover

Mommy Makeover In San Antonio TX is the combination of breast lift, tummy tuck, and liposuction. This is a common body contouring surgery that can be performed safely.

The usual risks apply, but the risk of blood clots (which can be life threatening) is higher with this combination.

Your surgeon will likely take precautions such as use of pnuematic compression hose (hose that pump the blood in and out of your legs) before, during, and after surgery, attention to keeping your body temperature from falling too low during mommy makeover, control of your pain, and early ambulation.

I also give my patients a blood thinning shot (such as Arixtra or Lovenox) for a few days after mommy makeover to further reduce the risk of blood clots.

There is also a recommended limit on the amount of total liposuction to help maintain the greatest degree of safety.

Discuss all of the above with your surgeon during your preop evaluation. (Michael D. Yates, MD, Huntsville Plastic Surgeon)

Mommy Makeover In San Antonio TX is the combination of Tummy Tucks and Liposuction. This is a common one.

The risk of complications in a young healthy person is low. But they do exist. There is no question that the risk increases with the length of mommy makeover as well as with additional procedures being added to a tummy tuck.

But, I think the risks need to be evaluated on a case by case basis. One also has to consider the risk of multiple anesthesias for multiple separate procedures. There are always risks.

The most important thing is to discuss these issues with your doctor about your personal situation and follow all of your doctors instructions and take every precaution to prevent complications. (Daniel P. Markmann, MD, Baltimore Plastic Surgeon)

When deciding on how to combine multiple procedures, you have to think about safety. Time is always a factor with mommy makeover , and you can see from multiple posts that most of us agree that elective procedures should be restricted to less than 6 hours to minimize the chance of a DVT / PE.

A PE is one of the most catastrophic complication that a healthy patient might encounter, so all efforts should be taken to avoid it. You need to ask your selected surgeon how long they would expect to take to perform the procedural combinations you are considering with this in mind.

Mommy Make Over Cosmetic In San Antonio Combines Breast Enhancement And Body Contouring

A tummy tuck and breast lift is a frequent combination that can easily be performed in less than six hours by most surgeons and would be a smart combination to start your makeover.

The liposuction is a bit more complicated – to address all of the regions you are requesting, you will need to be repositioned several times during surgery (which adds time to the procedure).

Additionally, there is some literature that suggests DVT risks are higher when combining lower extremity lipo with a tummy tuck, so I would strongly discourage this combination. If you were my patient, I’d suggest starting with the breast lift and tummy tuck, and later coming back to perform the liposuction in all those regions. (Michael A. Bogdan, MD, FACS, Dallas Plastic Surgeon)

Mommy Makeover At Decherd Plastic Surgery

The combination of procedures you mention is the classic mommy makeover and is done all the time. Factors that contribute to risk are medical conditions, smoking, obesity, and the length of the surgery.

For healthy people, I limit the length of the operation to under 6 hours. There is good data to support the safety of combining procedures, though it has been controversial in the past, due primiarily to too much time under anesthesia in my opinion. (Richard Baxter, MD, Seattle Plastic Surgeon)

The amount of mommy makeover that is possible and safe would depend on several variables. Your overall state of health The anticipated length of the proposed operation when performed by your chosen surgeon (we all have our own pace in surgery)

The recovery restrictions anticipated with the procedures you are interested in (you would not want to have a procedure that requires you to lie on your back combined with a procedure the outcome of which might suffer if you spent too much time on your back).

I would ask you chosen surgeon this question, and if you are still uncertain, ask your family doctor as well. (Armando Soto, MD, FACS, Orlando Plastic Surgeon)

Breast Lift, Tummy Tuck and Liposuction Combined
It’s very common to combine these three procedures. Typically as long as the mommy makeover is done safely it’s not an issue. Meaning that blood clot prevention is undertaken, the patient is positioned and padded appropriately and they are at optimum health. Time is the only other factor. San Antonio mommy makeover really should not exceed 8 hours. But these three procedures can be done under this. (Christopher L. Hess, MD, Fairfax Plastic Surgeon)
