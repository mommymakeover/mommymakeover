A Mommy Makeover in San Jose is a very broad all encompassing term which generally refers to surgery to improve the appearance of a women’s body following childbirth.

San Jose Mommy makeover with/without breast implants

Many times the female body undergoes changes after pregnancy . The mommy makeover consists of a combination of procedures that aim to restore the good appearance of the woman body through surgical techniques.

Each patient is different , therefore the combination of these procedures are adequate to the needs of the patient.

Typically combines abdominoplasty ( to remove the excess skin on the abdomen and reposition the rectus abdominal muscles ) with lipo sculpture (to remove localized fat in the abdomen , back and flanks ) and lifting of the breasts.

Depending on the consistency and volume of the mammary gland , it will be necessary or not the inclusion of an implant and thus achieve more aesthetic results. When the breasts are very empty and have much flab , we recommend the inclusion of implants to fill and give projection.

The difference between lifting with implants or not, lies in the cost of the prosthesis , which must be added to the total cost of the procedure and of course, the increase of surgical time.

The cost of these procedures separately is usually more expensive than them all in a single intervention.

In our country the cost of a Mommy makeover with implants cost 6500 USD and without implants around 5800 USD .Wherever you decide to become the procedure, find a certified surgeon. (Javier Baez Angles, MD, Dominican Republic Plastic Surgeon)

Combining tummy tuck, breast augmentation & liposuction into one surgery

If you are healthy, without any significant medical conditions and working with an experienced plastic surgeon, then performing all three procedure at once is certainly reasonable and something that I advise my patients to do for 3 reasons: The post operative healing process is limited to a single experience.

The overall cost can be lowered by doing everything in one sitting. Risks associated with anesthesia are limited to a single session. These procedures have minimal blood loss and generally require approximately 4-5 hours to perform.
