Recovery from a Mommy Makeover

Discomfort is subjective, and also depends on how your surgeon manages your pain control. The fact that you did OK with the c-sections really goes in your favor.

The tummy tuck component of the makeover is the part that is most uncomfortable, to the point where you will likely not really notice the breast surgery that much.

Make sure you follow your surgeons instructions carefully (the first 2 days are the most uncomfortable, so if you are instructed to take medications at certain times, stay on the schedule!), and then discomfort typically drops down to a “nagging” level by 5 to 7 days.

I don’t think you will scare your children, but have your husband tell them to give you some quiet time for the first several days. (Michael A. Bogdan, MD, FACS, New York Plastic Surgeon)

Recovery from Breast Augmentation and Tummy Tuck

The first few days will be the worst. Most of the pain will be from the muscle repair part of the tummy tuck.

You will feel liek you were hit by a truck for the first few days, but by a week, you willbe sore but it will be bearable.

Most of my patients are able to return to work at 2 weeks, as long as no heavy lifting is involved.

As many people know, pain is very different between people, so there is no definite rule. (Matthew Schulman, MD, Orange County Plastic Surgeon)

In our practice we find the vast majority of patients have a relatively quick recovery, however this depends from patient to patient. There are very few patients who have complained of “unbearable” pain that you describe. You may want to discuss your fears of pain to your surgeon as there are measures they can take and discuss with your anesthesia provider.

In general you should plan on some tightness at your breasts from the augmentation with lift. Also remember that where your breasts will be positioned just after surgery is not indicative of where they’ll be in six months. The tummy tuck may be more painful, however plan to walk bending over for the first few days.

Your compression garments will help with your swelling, and you may benefit from sleeping the first few nights in a recliner chair (position your body in the beach chair position). We find this helps our patients with the tightness in their abdomen.

Since we always see our tummy tuck, breast augmentation and mommy makeover patients the very next day, I find the patients appreciate discussing how they’re feeling immediately after the procedure.

Remember to do your best to “stay ahead” of the pain and do not hesitate to contact your surgeon if you are concerned. They will appreciate the open communication. (Brian Coan, MD, FACS, Dominican Republic Plastic Surgeon)
